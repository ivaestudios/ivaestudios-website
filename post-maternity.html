Maternity at the Resort — a restrained editorial approach.

Pregnancy is brief and unrepeatable. The portraits should be as well — considered, restrained, and made for the wall, not the feed. Below is how the studio approaches a maternity session at a luxury resort in the Mexican Caribbean.

The gestational window

The studio works between weeks 28 and 34. The bump reads clearly in flowing fabric. Energy is still relatively high. Most pregnancy-related discomfort has not yet narrowed what feels comfortable on a beach. The silhouette is unmistakably itself.

Earlier sessions, in the 24 to 26 week range, can be considered for first pregnancies — when the bump arrives later. Later sessions, after week 34, are accommodated case by case, with shorter walks, fewer transitions, and proximity to the room. Twins and multiples are best photographed two weeks earlier than the standard window.

Travel timing aligns naturally with this window. Most international carriers permit travel through weeks 32 to 34, so the 28-to-32 segment is the practical sweet spot for guests flying in.

Wardrobe — flowing silhouettes, nothing tight

The rule is simple: nothing tight at the waist, nothing constrictive across the rib cage, nothing that competes with the shape of the bump. The body has done its own composition; the fabric should let it speak.

What works:

  • Long chiffon or silk gowns with empire waists or wrap closures.
  • Draped jersey with an open or one-shoulder neckline.
  • Soft linen for the partner — natural fibers, ivory or sand.
  • A second, sheer layer for movement in the evening breeze.

The palette is restrained — ivory, cream, blush, sand, soft sage, dusty blue. Deep jewel tones (emerald, burgundy) read well at sunset when warmth saturates them. Stark white overexposes against pale sand. Heavy patterns compete with the figure. The studio sends a wardrobe brief with mood references after booking.

The hour — sunset, not sunrise

The default for maternity is the final golden hour before sundown. The light is longer, warmer, and arrives after the heat of the day has eased — which matters when the subject is in her third trimester. Sunrise sessions are reserved for the warmest months, when the late hour pushes against fatigue.

Practically, this means a 90-minute window beginning roughly an hour before sunset. The studio scouts the angle in advance and times each transition to the light, not the other way around.

Where we shoot (and where we don't)

The session begins at the resort. Infinity pools, garden paths, and the private stretch of beach attached to the property cover most of what a maternity gallery needs — and they cost nothing in walking distance, shade, water, or proximity to a bathroom. For couples who want a second register, an open beach within ten minutes of the property adds horizon and sky.

Cenotes are off the table. They appear in other sessions for couples and families, but not for maternity. Uneven access, cool water, limited evacuation, and slick limestone are not a fair trade for the image. The studio declines them on principle for expectant clients — the same way it declines deep boats and long jungle hikes during this window. The resort and the open beach do the work without the risk.

For sessions in Cancún, the Hotel Zone resorts and Playa Delfines provide the open coastline. Across Riviera Maya, the private beaches at Mayakoba, Maroma and Tulum's hotel strip read more editorial. Los Cabos trades turquoise for the warm, sculpted light of the Pacific — the silhouette work is exceptional there.

Including the partner and older children

Maternity is rarely a solo portrait in practice. The session is structured in acts — solo portraits of the mother, then quiet couple frames, then a short family pass with older children at the end, when their energy is best spent. We do not stage the children; we direct the parents, and the children find their own way in.

If the trip already includes a wider family session for older siblings or grandparents, the maternity hour can attach to it cleanly — one continuous piece of direction rather than two separate visits.

Booking and what comes next

The studio is now booking maternity sessions for 2026 across Riviera Maya, Cancún and Los Cabos. The starting point is your travel dates and the resort name — that's enough for a first response, usually within 24 hours, with availability and a session structure tailored to the property and the gestational week.

Galleries are delivered within two to three weeks. A short preview — three to five images — arrives within 48 hours, suitable for an early announcement if you want one. Send the brief through the contact page when you're ready.

Frequently asked

When is the right window?
Weeks 28 to 34. The bump reads clearly in flowing fabric, energy is still high, and the silhouette is unmistakable. Earlier for twins, slightly later for first pregnancies.
Why sunset rather than sunrise?
The final hour before sundown gives a longer, warmer light and arrives after the day's heat has eased. Sunrise is reserved for the warmest months when fatigue at evening becomes a factor.
What about cenotes?
Off the table for maternity. Uneven access, cool water, limited evacuation routes — not a fair trade for the image. The studio works the resort and the open beach instead.
What should the wardrobe look like?
Flowing silhouettes — long chiffon or silk gowns, draped jersey, empire waists. Nothing tight at the waist or the rib cage. The studio sends a wardrobe brief with a curated palette after booking.
Can my partner and older children join?
Yes. The session is structured in acts: solo portraits, couple frames, a short family pass with older children at the end, when their energy is best spent.
