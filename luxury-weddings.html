The brief

A wedding is two to four hours of real light. We plan around it.

Most resort galleries treat the wedding day as a checklist. Ours treats it as an editorial spread — selected angles, considered light, two to four hours that hold together as a single piece of work. The first dance, the bridal preparation, the readings — these are the spine. Around them, the day unfolds.

We accept one wedding per weekend. Multi-location days (resort to cenote to beach) are accommodated with a planned vehicle window and a portrait stop at last light. The studio operates as a single editorial eye unless the brief explicitly calls for a second.

Scope of coverage

Three coverage scopes. No package gymnastics.

Investment is quoted against the brief, not against a tiered menu. The three scopes below describe how we cover the day — what they cost is sent privately after we read your timeline.

01

Ceremony only

Ceremony coverage starts at the bridal preparation and ends with the first dance. The cinematic spine of the day, distilled. Suited to elopements and intimate guest counts where the focus is the vow itself.

Two hours, one location

02

Ceremony plus portraits

Continuous ceremony coverage with a dedicated portrait window built around golden hour. Twenty to forty minutes of intentional couple portraits at the day's best light, set apart from the documentary flow.

Three hours, two scenes

03

Full event, golden-hour add-on

Documentary coverage from preparation through the speeches, with a separate portrait window held in reserve at last light. The most flexible scope — suited to multi-location days and resort venues with multiple ceremony spaces.

Four hours, multi-location

Process

From brief to delivered gallery.

Four steps from first email to the final edit. Wedding planning has its own rhythm — ours is built to interlock with yours, not compete with it.

  1. i

    Pre-event call

    A thirty-minute conversation, video or audio. We read your timeline, hear the brief, and answer the practical questions — weather contingencies, vendor coordination, the specific quality you want the photographs to hold.

  2. ii

    Day-of timeline coordination

    We coordinate directly with your wedding planner four to eight weeks out. A short shot-mix is agreed in writing; the rest is composed live. The portrait window is reserved against sunset, never against the schedule.

  3. iii

    The wedding day

    Single photographer unless the brief requires more. We arrive thirty minutes early to scout final light. Multi-location days are run with a planned vehicle window. Weather contingency is decided live with the planner; we never default to umbrellas if a covered architectural setting will hold.

  4. iv

    Preview & final delivery

    A curated preview set of forty to sixty edited images arrives within seven days — for the announcement, the thank-you cards, the first prints. The complete gallery is delivered in six to eight weeks, hand-graded.

FAQ

Four practical questions.

How far in advance should we book?
We accept a single wedding per weekend. November through April is peak season at luxury resorts in Riviera Maya, Cancún and Los Cabos — couples typically book six to ten months ahead. Off-peak dates can sometimes be held with eight to twelve weeks of lead time. Once dates are confirmed, the calendar closes for that weekend.
Do you bring a second photographer?
By default, no. The studio is built around a single editorial eye, and most resort weddings are well covered without a second body. We add a second photographer only when the brief calls for parallel coverage — first looks happening simultaneously in different rooms, large guest counts above one hundred and twenty, or a venue with two separate ceremony spaces. We will tell you before booking if your day requires one.
Do you offer wedding video?
Photography is the studio's discipline; we do not film weddings ourselves. For couples who want both, we collaborate with two cinematographers in the region whose visual register matches ours. We coordinate angles and timing so neither team competes for the same frame, and you receive both deliverables on a coherent grade. Introductions happen during the pre-event call.
How does gallery delivery work for a wedding?
Two stages. Within seven days of the wedding, you receive a curated preview set of forty to sixty edited images — selected for the announcement, the thank-you cards, the first prints. The complete edited gallery follows in six to eight weeks, hand-graded image by image, delivered through a private gallery with full download. Total volume sits between four hundred and seven hundred images depending on coverage scope.
