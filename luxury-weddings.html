Wedding Day Timeline Guide

Designed around the light

The key to extraordinary wedding photography in Mexico is scheduling around natural light. Below is our recommended timeline for a winter wedding in Cancún or Riviera Maya (sunset approximately 5:45 PM). We adjust this for each couple based on season, venue, and ceremony style.

12:00 PM

Getting Ready Begins

Hair, makeup, and bridal details. We photograph the dress, shoes, rings, invitation suite, and the moments of anticipation as the day unfolds.

2:00 PM

First Look (Optional)

A private first look before the ceremony provides 20 to 30 minutes of relaxed couple portraits in beautiful afternoon light, before the emotions of the ceremony.

3:30 PM

Pre-Ceremony & Guest Arrival

Ceremony details, venue atmosphere, guest arrivals, and the final moments before the processional. The light at this hour is warm and directional, ideal for documentary images.

4:00 PM

Ceremony

Scheduled to finish approximately 90 minutes before sunset, giving us the full arc of golden hour for portraits. The late afternoon sun provides soft, flattering illumination for the ceremony itself.

4:45 PM

Family & Bridal Party Portraits

Efficient, organized group portraits while the light is still even and bright. We provide a shot list template in advance to keep this session moving smoothly.

5:15 PM

Golden Hour Couple Portraits

The most magical window of the day. Warm, amber light pouring across the beach or through the architecture of your venue. This is when the most iconic images of your wedding day are created.

5:45 PM

Sunset & Cocktail Hour

Dramatic sunset silhouettes transition into the relaxed atmosphere of cocktail hour. We capture candids of your guests enjoying the evening as the sky transforms.

7:00 PM

Reception & Celebration

Grand entrance, first dance, toasts, dinner, and dancing. We use a refined lighting approach that maintains the ambiance of your reception while delivering clean, beautifully lit images throughout the night.

For summer weddings, shift the timeline approximately 90 minutes later. Los Cabos sunsets occur slightly earlier than the Caribbean coast. We provide a customized timeline for every couple based on their specific date and venue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

How much does a destination wedding photographer in Mexico cost?

Our destination wedding photography packages range from $3,500 to $12,000 USD. The Essential Collection starts at $3,500 and includes 8 hours of coverage with one photographer. Our Signature Collection includes full-day coverage, a second shooter, and an engagement session. The Grand Collection adds cinematic videography and a highlight film. Each collection is customizable to match your specific needs and wedding scale.

How far in advance should I book my wedding photographer?

We recommend booking 8 to 12 months before your wedding date, particularly for peak season dates between November and April. We accept a limited number of weddings per month to ensure every couple receives our full creative attention. Early booking also allows time for engagement sessions and detailed venue scouting before your wedding day.

Do you travel to all resort areas in Mexico?

Our team covers three primary destinations: the Cancún Hotel Zone, the complete Riviera Maya corridor from Puerto Morelos through Tulum, and Los Cabos including Cabo San Lucas and San José del Cabo. Travel within these regions is included in all wedding packages at no additional cost. We are also available for weddings at venues outside these areas with a travel supplement.

What is the best time of year for a destination wedding in Mexico?

The dry season from November through April offers the most reliable weather, with temperatures around 80°F (27°C) and low humidity. December through March is peak wedding season. For the Caribbean coast, June and July offer excellent conditions with fewer tourists. Hurricane season runs June through November, with September and October posing the highest risk. Los Cabos has a slightly different weather pattern with less rain overall.

What time should our ceremony start for the best photos?

We recommend scheduling your ceremony to conclude approximately 90 minutes before sunset. In Cancún and Riviera Maya, golden hour occurs around 5:30 PM in winter months and 7:00 PM in summer. A 4:00 PM winter ceremony or 5:30 PM summer ceremony allows time for the ceremony, couple portraits during golden hour, and stunning sunset reception photos. We provide a detailed, customized timeline for every couple.

How many photos will we receive?

Depending on your collection, you will receive between 400 and 800+ editorially graded photographs. Every image receives our signature color treatment. Your gallery is delivered within 6 to 8 weeks through a private online platform with full-resolution downloads and social sharing. Sneak peek images are delivered within 72 hours of your wedding.

Can we schedule an engagement session before the wedding?

Absolutely. Engagement and bridal sessions are included in our Signature and Grand Collections. Popular locations include jungle cenotes, the Tulum beachfront ruins, downtown Cancún, and your resort grounds. Many couples schedule this session for the day before the wedding, combining it with their rehearsal day. It builds camera comfort and produces additional art for your home.

Do you include a second photographer for weddings?

Our Signature and Grand Collections include a second photographer as standard. A second shooter captures parallel moments such as both getting-ready experiences, dual ceremony angles, and guest candids during key moments. For intimate elopements or micro-weddings under 30 guests, a single photographer may provide all the coverage needed.

Do you offer wedding videography as well?

Yes. Our Grand Wedding Collection includes cinematic videography alongside full-day photography. This includes a 3 to 5 minute highlight film and a complete ceremony edit. Having one team handle both photo and video ensures seamless coordination, no competing for angles, and a cohesive visual style across all your wedding media.

What happens if it rains on our wedding day?

Our team is experienced in adapting to tropical weather. Most luxury resorts offer elegant indoor backup locations, and we scout these during our pre-wedding venue visit. Afternoon rain showers in Mexico are typically brief, lasting 20 to 40 minutes. Some of our most dramatic and romantic wedding images have been captured immediately after the rain passes, when the sky fills with extraordinary color and soft, diffused light.
