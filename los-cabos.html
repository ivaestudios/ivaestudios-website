Why IVAE in Los Cabos

Where the Desert Meets the Pacific

Los Cabos is unlike any other destination in Mexico. The Baja desert plunges into the Sea of Cortez and Pacific Ocean, creating dramatic granite cliffs, windswept dunes, and turquoise waters that feel otherworldly. As a Cabo photographer, our team uses this raw, cinematic geography as part of your story.

Pacific-Side Golden Light

The west-facing coastline produces some of the most spectacular golden-hour light in the Western Hemisphere. Unobstructed Pacific sunsets cast long amber light that wraps around subjects and ignites the landscape in warm, honeyed tones. We know precisely where to position you for maximum impact across every season.

A Cinematic Landscape

From the granite arches of Land's End to San Jose del Cabo's art district, Los Cabos offers visual range few destinations match. Within a single session we move from a private infinity pool to cobblestone gallery lanes, delivering images that feel like a short film.

Everything You Need to Know

Professional luxury photography in Los Cabos typically ranges from $500 to $2,500 USD depending on session length, number of locations, and deliverables. A standard 90-minute golden-hour session at one or two resort locations includes professional editing, a private online gallery, and high-resolution digital files. Weddings and multi-day coverage are quoted individually based on scope.
Los Cabos enjoys over 340 days of sunshine per year, making it exceptional for photography year-round. October through May offers the most consistent weather with warm temperatures and minimal rain. The winter months bring the most dramatic Pacific sunsets and humpback whale sightings in the background of coastal images. Summer months are warmer but produce spectacular cloud formations at golden hour.
Yes. Our team regularly photographs at the top luxury resorts throughout Los Cabos, including Waldorf Astoria Los Cabos Pedregal, One&Only Palmilla, Esperanza, The Cape, Chileno Bay, Nobu Hotel Los Cabos, Montage Los Cabos, and Solaz. We understand each property's photography policies and coordinate with resort management in advance so your session is seamless.
We recommend booking four to eight weeks in advance for standard sessions and six to twelve months for weddings or peak-season dates from December through March. Last-minute availability is sometimes possible depending on our schedule, so it is always worth inquiring even on shorter timelines.
The golden hour — roughly 45 minutes before sunset — is the most coveted window for photography in Cabo. The Pacific-side light produces an exceptionally warm, amber glow that wraps around subjects and illuminates the desert landscape in ways unique to this coastline. Sunrise sessions are also stunning with near-empty beaches and softer, rosier light.
Absolutely. Our team specializes in discreet proposal coverage at iconic Los Cabos locations. Popular spots include private sunset dinners on the beach at Pedregal, the cliffside terraces at The Cape, and private yacht charters with views of El Arco. We coordinate with resort concierge teams and event planners to capture the moment authentically while remaining invisible until the surprise is revealed.
A 60-minute session delivers 50 to 80 professionally edited images; a 90-minute session delivers 80 to 120. Wedding coverage yields 400 to 800 or more images depending on hours of coverage. Your private online gallery is typically delivered within two to three weeks. Every image is individually color-graded to our signature editorial style — warm, luminous, and cinematic.
We recommend neutral, earthy tones — sand, ivory, sage, terracotta, soft gold — that complement the desert-meets-ocean palette of Los Cabos. Flowing fabrics photograph beautifully in the coastal breeze. Avoid bold logos, neon colors, and overly busy patterns. For families, coordinate colors without matching exactly. We provide a detailed style guide after booking.
El Arco (The Arch) is best used as a dramatic backdrop rather than a walk-up location, as it is only accessible by water taxi or private boat. We can incorporate El Arco into a session by photographing from Lover's Beach with the arch in the background, or by arranging a private boat charter where we capture images on the water with the formation behind you. Both approaches produce breathtaking results.
