Why IVAE in Los Cabos

Where the Desert Meets the Pacific

Los Cabos is unlike any other destination in Mexico. The stark Baja desert plunges directly into the Sea of Cortez and the Pacific Ocean, creating a landscape of dramatic granite cliffs, windswept dunes, and turquoise waters that feels almost otherworldly. As a cabo photographer, our team understands how to use this raw, cinematic geography as more than a backdrop. It becomes part of your story.

Pacific-Side Golden Light

The west-facing coastline of Cabo San Lucas produces some of the most spectacular golden-hour light in the Western Hemisphere. The unobstructed Pacific horizon means sunsets here are not merely beautiful but theatrical, casting long amber light that wraps around subjects and ignites the desert landscape in warm, honeyed tones. Our team has studied this light across every season and knows precisely where to position you for maximum impact.

A Cinematic Landscape

From the weathered granite arches of Land's End to the pastel colonial facades of San Jose del Cabo's art district, Los Cabos offers a visual range that few destinations can match. Within a single session, we can move from a private infinity pool overlooking the Pacific to a centuries-old cobblestone gallery lane, delivering a portfolio of images that feels like a short film rather than a slideshow.

The IVAE Process

From Inquiry to Gallery

Our process is designed for international travelers who value simplicity, clear communication, and a seamless experience from the moment they reach out to the moment their gallery arrives. Every detail is handled so you can focus entirely on enjoying Los Cabos.

Everything You Need to Know

Professional luxury photography in Los Cabos typically ranges from $500 to $2,500 USD depending on session length, number of locations, and deliverables. A standard 90-minute golden-hour session at one or two resort locations includes professional editing, a private online gallery, and high-resolution digital files. Weddings and multi-day coverage are quoted individually based on scope.
Los Cabos enjoys over 340 days of sunshine per year, making it exceptional for photography year-round. October through May offers the most consistent weather with warm temperatures and minimal rain. The winter months bring the most dramatic Pacific sunsets and humpback whale sightings in the background of coastal images. Summer months are warmer but produce spectacular cloud formations at golden hour.

Our team regularly photographs at the top luxury resorts throughout Los Cabos, including Waldorf Astoria Los Cabos Pedregal, One&Only Palmilla, Esperanza, The Cape, Chileno Bay, Nobu Hotel Los Cabos, Montage Los Cabos, and Solaz. We recommend booking four to eight weeks in advance for standard sessions and six to twelve months for weddings or peak-season dates from December through March. Last-minute availability is sometimes possible depending on our schedule, so it is always worth inquiring even on shorter timelines.
The golden hour — roughly 45 minutes before sunset — is the most coveted window for photography in Cabo. The Pacific-side light in Los Cabos produces an exceptionally warm, amber glow that wraps around subjects and illuminates the desert landscape in ways unique to this coastline. Sunrise sessions are also stunning and offer near-empty beaches and softer, rosier light.

A 60-minute session delivers 50 to 80 professionally edited images; a 90-minute session delivers 80 to 120. Wedding coverage yields 400 to 800 or more images depending on hours of coverage. Your private online gallery is typically delivered within two to three weeks. Every image is individually color-graded to our signature editorial style — warm, luminous, and cinematic.
We recommend neutral, earthy tones — sand, ivory, sage, terracotta, soft gold — that complement the desert-meets-ocean palette of Los Cabos. Flowing fabrics photograph beautifully in the coastal breeze. Avoid bold logos, neon colors, and overly busy patterns. For families, coordinate colors without matching exactly. We provide a detailed style guide after booking.
El Arco (The Arch) is best used as a dramatic backdrop rather than a walk-up location, as it is only accessible by water taxi or private boat. We can incorporate El Arco into a session by photographing from Lover's Beach with the arch in the background, or by arranging a private boat charter where we capture images on the water with the formation behind you. Both approaches produce breathtaking results.
