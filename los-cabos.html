Destination · Baja California Sur

Los Cabos. Pacific drama.

The Pacific side of the trinity. El Arco, Lovers Beach, Pedregal cliffs, Palmilla. Bigger swells, harder light, more contrast — and resorts built for the view.

The region, briefly

Where the Pacific meets the Sea of Cortez. Cabo sits at the meeting line.

Granite cliffs, desert reflectance, and a horizon that turns amber forty minutes before it goes. Cabo is the most photogenic of the three regions when the light is right — and the most punishing at noon. Our work here is built around the hour the Pacific actually shows itself.

Where we shoot in Cabo

Five sub-areas, one coastline.

01

Cabo San Lucas · downtown & marina

The energy hub. Yacht charters depart from the marina at the south end of town. We use the marina at first light or late afternoon — earlier than the wedding-cruise crowds, later than the day-tour returns. Departure point for our El Arco sessions on water.

02

Pedregal · the cliff residences

A private hillside above Cabo San Lucas, accessed through a tunnel cut into the rock. Stone, cactus, deep ocean drop-offs and the most dramatic west-facing terraces in the region. Pacific sunsets here read like a magazine cover.

03

El Arco & Lovers Beach · by water

The iconic granite arch at Land's End — only reachable by panga or yacht. We schedule Lovers Beach sessions inside a tide-and-light window: water taxis from the marina, twenty-minute crossing, then the sandbar and the rock. The yacht service window is built around this.

04

The Corridor · Palmilla, Esperanza, Las Ventanas

The twenty-mile stretch of Highway 1 between Cabo San Lucas and San José del Cabo where the major resorts sit. Calmer beaches, swimmable bays, manicured gardens, white-stucco architecture. The default surface for family and couples sessions when the wind is up.

05

San José del Cabo · the art-walk town

Quieter, lower, older. Adobe walls in saturated colors, small galleries, the colonial mission, jacaranda in spring. We use it for editorial sessions that want texture and shade — and for couples who prefer story to spectacle.

A note on light

Cabo's light is the harshest of the three regions.

The desert reflects. Mid-day sun bounces off granite, sand and stucco at angles that flatten skin and crush shadow detail. We treat noon as a working window for open shade only — under loggia, against architecture, inside a courtyard. Never on open beach.

Golden hour here is brief and gorgeous. Because the resorts face west toward the Pacific, sunset is the better window for portraits with water in the frame — about forty minutes of useable amber, then the horizon goes. We arrive earlier than the call sheet says.

Working in Cabo

Four questions, asked often.

Pacific or Sea of Cortez?

Cabo straddles both. The Sea of Cortez side is calmer, more swimmable, and mostly faces east — better for sunrise frames. The Pacific side is wilder, with stronger swells and higher cliffs, and faces west — better for sunset portraits with water in the frame. We shoot more on the Pacific side because that is where the region's character lives.

Can we hold a wedding ceremony at El Arco?

Yes, in spirit. El Arco is reachable only by water — we coordinate a private boat or panga to Lovers Beach, where the ceremony can be held with the rock as the backdrop. Permits, tide windows and sound considerations are part of the planning. We work with local boat captains on a per-session basis.

How far is the airport from the resort?

Los Cabos International (SJD) sits between San José del Cabo and the Corridor. Most luxury resorts are 35 to 50 minutes by car. Pedregal and downtown Cabo San Lucas sit at the far end (closer to 50). Palmilla, Esperanza, Las Ventanas and Montage are roughly 35 to 40.

How does whale season impact the session?

From January through March, humpbacks pass close to shore on the Pacific side. We stage portraits looking out toward the water so a breach, when one happens, falls naturally inside the frame. We don't promise a whale — we make sure the composition is ready if one appears.
