Secrets vs. Dreams Resorts: Which Suits Your Wedding?

By Vianey Díaz · April 10, 2026 · 8 min read

Almost every week, a couple sends the studio a version of the same message: "We've narrowed it down to Secrets or Dreams, and we honestly can't decide." It is the most common fork in the road for couples marrying along this coast, and for good reason. Both brands belong to Hyatt's Inclusive Collection, both deliver the polished all-inclusive experience that makes a destination wedding feel effortless, and both photograph beautifully. But they are built for two different weddings, and after years of shooting at properties under each flag from Cancun down to Maroma, we have a clear, honest sense of who belongs where.

The same family, two personalities

It helps to start with what these brands actually are. Secrets and Dreams sit side by side in the same portfolio, managed by the same hospitality group that also runs Breathless, Zoëtry and Now. They share the wedding infrastructure that makes Mexican all-inclusives so easy to book from abroad: dedicated on-site wedding coordinators, tiered ceremony packages, in-house spa and salon teams, and the kind of unlimited food and drink that takes the catering headache off your plate entirely. So the choice is rarely about quality. It is about atmosphere.

The cleanest way to put it: Secrets is adults-only, and Dreams is for everyone. That single rule cascades into almost every difference that matters, from the soundtrack at the pool to who walks down the aisle. If you have spent any time reading about a luxury destination wedding in Cancun and the Riviera Maya, you have already met this decision under different names. With Hyatt's flagships, it is just unusually clear-cut.

Vibe and the kind of week you want

Secrets resorts, places like Secrets Maroma Beach Riviera Cancun, Secrets The Vine in the Cancun hotel zone, or Secrets Playa Mujeres, are designed for couples and grown-up celebration. The pools lean toward swim-up bars and unhurried afternoons, the dinners run later, and the overall hum is romantic rather than rowdy. For a wedding weekend, this means your golden-hour couple's session is genuinely calm. We can walk you down a quiet stretch of Maroma sand at 6 pm without weaving around a kids' water-aerobics class, and the resulting frames feel intimate and editorial.

Dreams resorts, such as Dreams Riviera Cancun, Dreams Natura near Puerto Morelos, or Dreams Playa Mujeres, keep all of that romance but add a genuine welcome for families. The signature Explorer's Club kids' program, splash parks, and connecting suites mean your nieces, nephews and your college roommate's toddler all have somewhere to be. The trade-off is energy: the main pool is livelier, mealtimes are busier, and the day has a different rhythm. For the right couple, that warmth is the entire point.

"The brand you choose is really a decision about who you want standing beside you when the sun goes down."

Ceremony venues and how they photograph

Both brands give you the trio of settings most couples want: a beach location with the Caribbean behind you, a garden or terrace option for shade and structure, and an indoor ballroom as a weather backup. Where they differ is the surrounding scenery during your portraits. Because Secrets properties skew adults-only, the beachfronts and infinity pools tend to read cleaner in wide shots, which matters enormously for the sweeping, unobstructed ceremony images couples ask us for. Secrets Maroma in particular sits on one of the most consistently beautiful beaches in the region, with soft white sand and shallow turquoise water that photographs like a postcard at almost any hour.

Dreams resorts answer with scale and architecture. Dreams Playa Mujeres, for instance, has dramatic infinity pools and a marina-adjacent feel, while Dreams Riviera Cancun offers a classic palapa-and-gazebo beach ceremony framed by palms. If your guest list runs large, the bigger Dreams footprints often handle a 60-to-120-person reception more gracefully. When couples weigh these against the boutique hotel-zone options, we usually walk them through our guide to the best Cancun wedding venues and, for the true high-luxury tier, properties like Le Blanc Spa Resort, so the comparison stays honest.

A photographer's tip

Whichever brand you choose, ask your coordinator for the latest ceremony start time the package allows, then book your couple's portraits for the 45 minutes before sunset. On this coast that golden window does more for your photos than any upgrade on the menu.

Guest mix and the message you send

This is the conversation we gently push couples to have early, because it shapes everyone's experience, not just yours. Choosing Secrets is, in effect, telling your guests this is an adult getaway. That is a wonderful filter for some weddings and a real complication for others. We have photographed couples who quietly delighted in a child-free week, and we have seen siblings scramble for off-site childcare because they assumed kids would be welcome. Neither outcome is wrong. It just needs to be a decision, not a surprise.

Dreams removes that friction entirely. Multigenerational groups, the kind where grandparents, parents and grandchildren all travel together, simply work better under this flag, and the resulting family frames are some of our favorite work. If your celebration leans that way, our approach to multigenerational family photography in Mexico is built precisely for these weekends, where the wedding is also a once-a-decade family reunion.

Budget and what your money actually buys

Couples often assume one brand is clearly cheaper. In practice, nightly rates and wedding-package pricing overlap heavily and depend far more on the specific property, the season, and how far ahead you book than on the Secrets-versus-Dreams label itself. A peak-season suite at a top Secrets property can cost more than a garden-view room at an older Dreams resort, and vice versa. The honest variables that move your total are room category, the number of nights your group commits to, and whether you trigger the free-private-event perks that both brands offer once your guests book enough room nights.

What we tell couples is this: pick the brand that fits your guest list and the feeling you want, then optimize the budget within it. Still on the fence? Let's talk it through

If you can describe your ideal Saturday night, you already know the answer. A long, candlelit dinner with adults laughing into the evening points to Secrets. A poolside afternoon with kids cannonballing while the grandparents watch from the shade points to Dreams. Both can host a genuinely beautiful, deeply photographable wedding, and we shoot happily at either.

As a bilingual studio based right here in Cancun, we have walked these beaches at every hour and know exactly where the light lands at each property. We will tell you honestly what we would choose, and then we will make it look the way you have been picturing it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Secrets resorts always adults-only and Dreams always family-friendly?

Yes, that is the core distinction. Secrets is an adults-only brand (typically 18 and over), while Dreams welcomes guests of all ages, including dedicated kids' clubs and family suites. It is the single most reliable way to tell them apart.

Can I have a large wedding at either brand?

You can, but the bigger Dreams properties like Dreams Playa Mujeres generally handle large receptions of 60 to 120 guests most comfortably. Secrets resorts host large weddings too; just confirm the venue capacity and private-event spaces with your coordinator before committing.

Is one brand more expensive than the other?

Not consistently. Pricing depends far more on the specific property, the season, room category and how early you book than on the Secrets-versus-Dreams label. We recommend choosing on vibe and guest list first, then optimizing your budget within that brand.

Do these resorts allow outside wedding photographers?

Most do, though some all-inclusives charge an outside-vendor fee or require your photographer to book a night on-site. We handle this often and can advise you on each property's current policy before you sign your contract.
