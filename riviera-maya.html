Best Locations

Where we photograph in Riviera Maya

Our team has scouted every major location and guides you to the spots that best match your vision and the light conditions on session day.

Cenotes

Riviera Maya's signature landscape. We photograph at Cenote Azul, Suytun, Dos Ojos, Ik Kil, and Zací -- each demanding different equipment and timing. Our team arrives prepared for every light condition.

Tulum Archaeological Zone

The only Maya ruins overlooking the sea. We arrive at 8 AM when the site opens, capturing images with soft eastern light illuminating El Castillo before midday crowds.

Playa del Carmen Beaches

Wide, powdery beaches with east-facing coastline ideal for sunrise sessions. Golden-hour light reflecting off calm waters creates extraordinary warmth in every frame.

Jungle Paths

Dense tropical jungle between the coast and cenotes provides lush canopy backdrops. Resort trails at Banyan Tree and Rosewood Mayakoba create an editorial, ethereal atmosphere.

Riviera Maya photography questions

Golden hour in Riviera Maya falls between 5:30 and 7:00 PM from November through March, and between 6:00 and 7:30 PM from April through October. For cenote sessions, midday (11 AM to 1 PM) is ideal because the overhead sun creates dramatic light beams through the cave openings. Our team selects the optimal time based on your specific locations and the season of your visit.
Yes. Our team photographs at several cenotes including Cenote Azul, Cenote Suytun, Cenote Ik Kil, Cenote Dos Ojos, and Cenote Zací. Each offers a distinct aesthetic, from open-air turquoise pools to underground cathedral-like caverns with natural light beams. We coordinate access and timing to ensure the best possible conditions for your session.

Yes. The Tulum archaeological zone, perched on cliffs above the Caribbean, is one of the most requested backdrops in Riviera Maya.
