The coast, briefly

A corridor of resorts, a system of cenotes, and the Caribbean on one side.

The Riviera Maya is the ninety-mile stretch from Puerto Morelos south to Tulum. It is where IVAE Studios shoots more sessions than any other coastline. The beach swings from the wide, crowd-thin sweep of Maroma down to the boutique-jungle aesthetic of Tulum, and just inland the limestone opens into a network of cenotes — open-air pools, half-domes, and full caves — that nothing else in the world matches.

The map · six chapters

One coast, several registers.

We choose the stretch by what the session is asking for. Below — what each one does best.

01

Tulum

The boutique-resort heart of the coast.

Beach-club aesthetic, jungle just behind the dunes. Hotel Bardo, Be Tulum, Casa Malca — the architectural register that pulls editorial photographers down here in the first place. Best when the brief leans intimate and design-forward.

02

Akumal

Quieter, slower, with sea-turtle season in summer.

Half-moon coves, light reef breaks, fewer visitors than Tulum or Playa. Sea-turtle window runs roughly May to October — we keep our distance from the protected zones and shoot the coast on either side.

03

Playa del Carmen

Town energy, with full 5th Avenue infrastructure.

The most accessible chapter — ferries to Cozumel from the pier, restaurants and street life on Quinta Avenida. Good for sessions that want a varied brief: beach in the morning, town in the late afternoon.

04

Mayakoba

A gated luxury campus — multiple resorts, one set of canals.

Andaz, Banyan Tree, Fairmont share the network of mangrove canals and a private beach behind a single guarded gate. We coordinate concierge access in advance — the property prefers a heads-up call before any external photographer arrives.

05

Maroma + Xpu-Ha

Wider beach, fewer crowds, the same turquoise.

Belmond Maroma anchors the north stretch; Xpu-Ha runs unhurried for a kilometer of soft sand south of it. When the session needs open beach without a single resort umbrella in the frame, we shoot here.

06

Cenotes

Pillars, shafts, and water that holds the light.

Gran Cenote, Dos Ojos, Calavera. The interior light is its own world — sunbeams enter the cavern openings between roughly 11 AM and 1 PM at Gran. We shoot the cenotes as a separate session window, not a stop on the way to the beach.

The light, considered

Softer than Cancún — the coast curves south-southeast.

01

Sunrise

Gold and slightly hazy. The inland humidity diffuses the first hour after the sun clears the horizon — the sky reads warmer and softer than in Cancún to the north.

02

Open shade

Once the sun crosses overhead, we move under the dune-edge palms or a resort's covered terrace. Open shade keeps skin tones natural without flattening the frame.

03

Sunset

Open shade rather than direct gold — diffused, west-facing, with the Caribbean reflecting blue back into the frame. The opposite register from Pacific Cabo, by design.

04

Cenote interior

Its own world. Pillars and shafts between roughly 11 AM and 1 PM at Gran Cenote, when the sun is steep enough to drop through the cavern opening. We schedule cenote sessions to that window — light first, everything else second.

Riviera Maya · Common questions

Four answers, specific to this coast.

Tulum vs Playa del Carmen — which for photos?

Tulum for the boutique-resort aesthetic — open beach, jungle-edge backdrops, the Bardo / Be Tulum / Casa Malca register. Playa del Carmen for energy and variety — 5th Avenue, the ferry, mid-morning street light. We choose by what the session is asking for, not by which town is more famous.

Cenotes — accessible all year?

Yes, but the water clarity follows the season. November through April the cenotes run their clearest — visibility is editorial. The rainy season (June through October) can muddy the inland-fed cenotes after a heavy storm. We adjust which cenote we shoot at based on the week's rainfall and the light angle that day.

How far from Cancún airport?

Roughly forty-five minutes from CUN to Playa del Carmen, ninety minutes to Tulum. Mayakoba and the Maroma stretch sit in between. Most guests arrive via private transfer; we coordinate session timing around the inbound flight so the first day isn't rushed.

Best resort for a couples session?

Anywhere along the Mayakoba and Maroma stretch — Andaz, Banyan Tree, Fairmont, Belmond Maroma, Rosewood. The beach is wide, the crowds are thin, and golden hour reads as private. Tulum's boutique resorts are the alternative if the brief leans editorial over expansive.
