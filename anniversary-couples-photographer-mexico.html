The Session

An anniversary, marked by just the two of you

An anniversary is one of the few occasions that asks for nothing but the two of you. No bridal party, no dinner to host, no guests to greet, only the couple who chose each other and have kept choosing. The studio photographs that quiet milestone the way it deserves to be remembered: an hour or two on a Mexican beach at golden hour, the two of you walking, leaning in, laughing at something only you understand. It is the wedding, distilled to its essential pair, photographed years later.

Many of the couples we photograph come back to where it all began. They were married in Cancún, on the Riviera Maya, in Tulum or Los Cabos, and they return on a fifth, tenth or twenty-fifth anniversary to stand on the same sand. We plan the session at that exact resort or beach, time it to the same light, and let the new frames answer the wedding ones. Other couples want somewhere they have never been, a first visit to a coastline they have always meant to see, and the session becomes the reason to finally go. Either way the work is the same: intimate, editorial, and built to feel timeless rather than dated to the year it was made.

This is, deliberately, a couple-only session. If your anniversary is a gathering, with children and grandchildren flown in, a dinner and toasts, that is a celebration we love but cover differently on our anniversary party photographer in Cancún page. For engagement, honeymoon and proposal sessions, see our broader couples photography page. This page is for the version where it is simply the two of you and the camera.

Light & Rhythm

How an anniversary session moves

Golden hour, the heart of it

The last 75 to 90 minutes before sunset, roughly 5:00 to 7:00 PM depending on the month, carry the warm, forgiving light that suits two people who have been together a long time. We build the whole session around that window.

Sunrise, for the empty beach

A sunrise session, around 5:30 to 7:00 AM, gives you a deserted shoreline and the stillest light of the day. For couples who married at sunrise, we time it to match, so the photographs rhyme with the originals.

Unhurried, by design

There is no run of show, no guests waiting, no schedule but the sun. The session is paced like a slow evening walk. That ease is what lets two people who feel shy at the start forget the camera is there by the end.

Movement, not poses

We direct with movement, walking, a slow dance in the shallows, a whispered word, rather than static poses. The bilingual direction guides every micro-adjustment so neither of you has to think about where to put your hands.

What To Expect

Wardrobe, light and the shape of the evening

An anniversary session asks very little of you. We meet at the agreed beach or resort terrace about ninety minutes before sunset, walk you through the first frames while the light is still high and bright, then let the session soften with the sky as it drops toward the water. There is nothing to memorize and no list of poses to get through. Most couples feel the camera disappear inside the first ten minutes, somewhere between the first walk and the first quiet laugh.

For wardrobe, the studio sends a short note after booking, and the guidance is simple. Choose two looks that coordinate without matching, in soft tones that hold golden light rather than fight it: cream, sand, warm taupe, dusty blue and gentle terracotta all photograph beautifully against Caribbean turquoise and Pacific gold. Flowing fabrics read well on the breeze, a longer dress moves with the wind, and barefoot on the sand almost always looks better than shoes. We steer gently away from large logos, neon and busy prints, and we are glad to look at your options before the day.

The light does most of the work, so the studio plans around it rather than against it. Golden hour on both coasts gives the warm, forgiving glow that flatters skin and softens every line, which is exactly why we never photograph an anniversary session in the flat, hard light of midday. On the Caribbean side the water glows turquoise into the last minutes of sun; on the Pacific in Los Cabos the light turns deeper and more amber as it falls behind the headland. You leave with a private online gallery of fully edited, color-graded images, delivered in one to three days, kept in our warm editorial tone so the photographs feel timeless rather than dated to the year they were made.

Considered Questions

Planning your anniversary session

Is an anniversary couples session just the two of us?

Yes. This session is built for the two of you and no one else: an hour or two of unhurried, romantic portraits at golden hour, no guests, no run of show, no audience. If your celebration involves children, grandchildren or friends who have travelled in, that is an anniversary party, and we cover it differently on our anniversary party page. This page is the couple-only session, intimate and quiet by design.

Can we return to the resort or beach where we married?

It is one of our favourite anniversary sessions to photograph. If you married in Cancún, the Riviera Maya, Tulum, Mayakoba or Los Cabos, we plan the session at that same beach, terrace or resort, often in the spot where you said your vows years before, and time it to the same light so the new frames answer the older ones.

How is this different from an anniversary party?

An anniversary couples session is the two of you alone, on the sand or terrace at golden hour. An anniversary party is the whole celebration: the dinner, the toasts, the children and grandchildren who flew in, the dancing. If you want only the two of you, this page is the right home. If there are guests, our anniversary party page in Cancún is built for that.

Is this the same as a vow renewal?

Not quite. A vow renewal centres on a ceremony, with words exchanged again, sometimes an officiant, often a few witnesses. An anniversary couples session has no ceremony; it is simply a romantic portrait session marking the years. Some couples do a quiet renewal at golden hour and then a session as one continuous evening, and we are glad to photograph both. For ceremonies and larger gatherings, see our event photography page.

Where in Mexico do you photograph anniversary sessions?

Across the Cancún Hotel Zone, Isla Mujeres, the Riviera Maya south through Mayakoba and Tulum, and the Pacific in Los Cabos. We work regularly at Rosewood Mayakoba, Le Blanc Spa Resort and Hyatt Ziva Cancún, and at quieter beaches and cenotes when you want somewhere private.

What is the best time of day for an anniversary photoshoot?

Golden hour, the last 75 to 90 minutes before sunset, roughly 5:00 to 7:00 PM depending on the month, gives the warm, soft Caribbean and Pacific light that suits an anniversary session. Sunrise, around 5:30 to 7:00 AM, is equally flattering and gives you an empty beach and the stillest light of the day; for couples who married at sunrise, we time the session to match.

We are shy in front of a camera. Will it feel natural?

Most couples we photograph have not faced a camera together since the wedding, and the direction is built for exactly that. We start with movement rather than poses, walking, a slow dance, a whispered word, so it feels like a private evening. The studio directs in English and Spanish, so neither of you has to think about where to put your hands.

Which anniversaries do couples usually book a session for?

Any of them. Tenth, twenty-fifth and fiftieth anniversaries are the most common, but we also photograph first and fifth anniversaries, the year a couple finally returns to their honeymoon coast, and milestones with no round number at all. The session marks the time you have spent together, whatever the figure on the card.

How far in advance should we book an anniversary session?

For the high season from November through April, and the winter holidays, reach out as soon as your travel dates are set, ideally several weeks ahead, because golden-hour slots fill first. Shorter notice is sometimes possible, especially for weekday sunrises. Send us your dates and the coastline, and the studio replies the same business day.

What should we wear for an anniversary session in Mexico?

Lean into soft, coordinated tones that hold the golden light rather than fight it: cream, sand, warm taupe, dusty blue and gentle terracotta photograph beautifully on Caribbean and Pacific sand. Flowing fabrics, linen and a longer dress catch the sea breeze on camera. After booking you receive a short wardrobe note, and we are glad to review your two looks before the session.

What is included, and how long is the session?

A standard anniversary couples session runs about one to two hours across one or two nearby settings at golden hour, with unhurried bilingual direction throughout and a private online gallery of fully edited, color-graded images delivered in one to three days. Permits or resort access, second locations and printed work can be added. Pricing is shared by inquiry once we know your dates and coastline.

Sunrise or sunset for an anniversary photoshoot?

Both are flattering; the choice is about mood and privacy. Sunset is warm, social and easy to build an evening around, with the beach at its most golden. Sunrise gives you an empty shoreline, cooler rose light and total quiet, ideal if you married at dawn or simply want the coast to yourselves. We will recommend one based on your resort, the season and the tide.

Can the session be a surprise for my partner?

Yes, and we plan these often. We coordinate the timing and meeting point quietly with you, frame the first moments so the reveal is part of the story, and direct gently so a partner who did not expect a camera settles within minutes. A surprise anniversary session pairs naturally with a quiet dinner reservation afterward, which we are happy to time around.
