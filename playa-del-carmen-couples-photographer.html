The Studio

A couples photographer who travels to Playa del Carmen

Playa del Carmen is the heart of the Riviera Maya: a stretch of Caribbean coast where the beach meets a walkable, lively town, where cenotes hide a few minutes inland, and where couples come to get engaged, to celebrate a honeymoon, or simply to be photographed somewhere that feels like a different life. It is one of the most romantic places we work. The light is warm, the water is turquoise, and the days end in long, slow sunsets made for two people walking the sand.

IVAE Studios is the studio that photographs those days. We are based in Cancún and travel to Playa del Carmen, about an hour south along the coast, as a matter of course. The Riviera Maya is home ground, so a Playa del Carmen session is a short, familiar drive for us rather than a special trip, with no significant travel surcharge. We arrive knowing the Playacar beaches, the cenotes nearby, and how the light moves along Quinta Avenida at the end of the day.

This is a couples service in the fullest sense. Whether you are newly engaged, planning a discreet proposal, on your honeymoon, or marking an anniversary, the session is built the same careful way: a planning conversation, a scouted location or two, a golden-hour call time, and a private gallery edited in our warm, restrained style. If you want one studio to hold this chapter of your story, you are in the right place.

Led by Director Vianey Díaz, the studio works in two languages and a single visual register: warm, restrained color, the honest light of the last hour before sunset, and direction quiet enough that the two of you forget the camera is there. This work sits inside our wider couples photography practice across the Riviera Maya, alongside engagements, proposals, honeymoons and anniversaries.

What It Includes

What a couples session includes

Every session is built the same careful way, whether it is an engagement, a proposal, a honeymoon or simply the two of you. Six things sit inside the service, and a few extras can be added by inquiry to suit your plan.

01

A Planning Conversation

Before anything is booked we talk through your dates, your story and the feeling you want. From there we recommend locations in and around Playa del Carmen, a call time and a loose flow, so the session feels considered rather than improvised on the day.

02

Location Scouting

We propose one or two settings matched to your mood: a Playacar beach, a shaded cenote, the color of Quinta Avenida. We know access, light and the quiet hours at each, and we plan the route so nothing feels rushed between them.

03

Golden-Hour Timing

The session is scheduled around the light, not the calendar. We hold the most important frames for the warm window near sunset, or the still sunrise hour, when the Playa del Carmen coastline is at its most flattering and least crowded.

04

Bilingual Direction

On the day we direct gently in English or Spanish. The aim is movement, not stiff poses: a walk along the sand, a turn, a quiet word between you. Couples who feel awkward in front of a camera tend to relax within the first few minutes.

05

Two Looks & Wardrobe Guidance

The session includes a second outfit and the styling notes to make both work, often one relaxed for the beach and one a touch more elevated for Quinta Avenida. We send guidance after booking and gladly review your choices so your looks suit the warm Caribbean palette.

06

A Private Gallery

After a careful, individual edit, your images arrive in a private high-resolution gallery with a print release. Delivery is timed so the photographs reach you in good time, whether for save-the-dates, an album, or simply to relive the trip.

Where We Photograph

The best couples locations in Playa del Carmen

01

Playacar Beaches

South of the ferry pier, the Playacar beaches are calmer and more private than the town stretch: soft sand, swaying palms and an open turquoise horizon. They are the classic Playa del Carmen backdrop most couples picture first, and they hold golden hour beautifully.

02

Riviera Maya Cenotes

The freshwater sinkholes a few minutes inland from Playa del Carmen. Limestone walls, hanging roots and pooled light make a cinematic, intimate counterpoint to the open beach. See our cenote photography for the full setting.

03

Quinta Avenida (Fifth Avenue)

The pedestrian heart of town, lively with color, cafés and architecture. Quinta Avenida suits couples who want energy and a sense of place in the gallery, a contrast to the quiet sand a block away.

04

Mamitas Beach

One of the most recognizable stretches in Playa del Carmen, with its wide sand and beach-club rhythm. Photographed early or at sunset, when the crowds thin, Mamitas reads warm and unmistakably Caribbean.

05

Sunset on the Sand

The single best window of the day. As the sun drops, the beach turns gold and empties, and the two of you have the shoreline almost to yourselves. This is where the frames couples treasure most tend to land.

06

Resort Grounds & Mayakoba

If you are staying at a resort in or near Playa del Carmen, your own grounds can be the setting: private beaches, manicured paths, water suites. The nearby Mayakoba estates, reached by boat, are a quiet, polished favorite.

When & What Light

When to do it, and how we plan the light

Two things decide when a session happens: where you are in your trip or your engagement, and where the light sits in the day. We plan around both so the photographs arrive looking the way Playa del Carmen looks at its best.

While you are in town

Most couples photograph during their stay, often on the first clear evening, so they have the rest of the trip to enjoy. Tell us where you are staying in or near Playa del Carmen and we build the session around the coast nearest you.

Around an engagement or wedding

If the session is tied to an engagement or a destination wedding, we time it three to nine months ahead for save-the-dates, or in the days before the wedding as a relaxed rehearsal in front of the camera.

Golden hour, first

The last 75 to 90 minutes before sunset, roughly 5:00 to 7:00 PM depending on the month, is when the Playa del Carmen beach turns warm and thins out. We hold the frames that matter most for this window, and shape the whole session around it.

Quiet sunrise

The east-facing Playa del Carmen sand is emptiest just after first light, around 6:00 to 7:00 AM. For couples who want the beach almost to themselves, a sunrise session trades an early alarm for a calm, private coastline and soft, even light.

Wardrobe & Styling

Wardrobe and the golden-hour palette

What you wear matters as much as where you stand. The Caribbean has a palette of its own, and a little planning lets your looks sit inside it rather than fight it. Here is how we guide wardrobe for a Playa del Carmen session.

01

Coordinate, Don't Match

Two looks that complement each other read better than identical outfits. Pair one relaxed look with one more elevated, in tones that sit beside each other. We help you build the combination so the two of you look like a pair, not a uniform.

02

Fabric That Moves

Flowing dresses, light linen and soft layers come alive in the sea breeze on the Playacar beaches. Movement is what makes a couples frame feel alive, so we steer toward fabrics that catch the wind rather than stiff, structured pieces that stay flat.

03

Warm, Soft Tones

Creams, sand, terracotta, soft blues and muted greens echo the coastline and hold up beautifully at golden hour. We tend to steer away from bright neons and busy logos, which pull the eye away from your faces and the light.

04

One Look for the Sea, One for the Town

If you are pairing a Playacar beach with Quinta Avenida or a cenote, we suggest a look for each. Breezy and barefoot for the sand, a touch more structured for the street or the limestone, so each setting gets the wardrobe it deserves.

05

Details and Comfort

The ring, of course, but also the small things: a hat for the sun, sandals you can walk in, a wrap for the cenote chill. We flag what to bring so nothing is missing, and so you are comfortable enough to forget the logistics.

06

We Review Before the Day

Send photos of your shortlisted looks and we will give honest, gentle feedback before you travel. It is far easier to adjust a wardrobe over a message than to wish you had on the beach, so we make that part of planning.

Considered Questions

What couples ask before booking

Are you based in Playa del Carmen?

We are based in Cancún and travel to Playa del Carmen, about an hour south along the coast, as a matter of course. The Riviera Maya is home ground for us, so a Playa del Carmen session is a short, familiar drive rather than a special trip, and there is no significant travel surcharge for it.

How long is a couples session in Playa del Carmen?

A standard couples session runs about ninety minutes to two hours, enough for one or two locations at a relaxed pace. Couples who want a Playacar beach, a cenote and a wardrobe change often book a longer half-day. We tailor the length to your plan.

How many photos do we receive?

A single-location session typically delivers a curated gallery of around eighty to one hundred and twenty finished images. A longer half-day across several settings delivers more. Every photo is individually edited in our warm, restrained style, not bulk filtered, and delivered in high resolution with a print release.

Where do you photograph couples in Playa del Carmen?

The Playacar beaches south of the ferry pier, the Riviera Maya cenotes inland, the pedestrian energy of Quinta Avenida (Fifth Avenue), Mamitas Beach, and the open sand at sunset. We match the location to the mood you want, from quiet and editorial to lively and full of color.

Can you photograph a surprise proposal in Playa del Carmen?

Yes. A discreet proposal is one of the most common reasons couples reach out. We scout a spot, position ourselves at a distance so the moment stays private, capture the question and the yes as it happens, and then flow into a relaxed couples session once the surprise is over. Mamitas Beach and the Playacar sand at sunset are favorites.

When is the best season, and what about sargassum?

November through April brings dry, clear weather and the calmest beaches. Sargassum seaweed can reach Caribbean shores between roughly April and August. We plan around it by checking current beach conditions in Playa del Carmen and, when needed, moving the session to a cenote, Quinta Avenida or a stretch of beach that stays clean that day.

What should we wear for a Playa del Carmen session?

Choose two coordinated looks rather than matching outfits: one relaxed for the beach and one a touch more elevated for Quinta Avenida or a cenote. Flowing fabrics read beautifully in the sea breeze, and soft, warm tones suit the Caribbean palette. We send styling guidance after booking and gladly review your choices before the day.

Do you shoot honeymoon sessions in Playa del Carmen?

Yes. Many couples book a honeymoon or anniversary session during their stay, often at their resort in or near Playa del Carmen, on a Playacar beach, or in a cenote nearby. It is the same editorial, golden-hour approach, simply aimed at the two of you on a trip rather than before a wedding.

Can we include a cenote in the session?

Yes. The Riviera Maya cenotes inland from Playa del Carmen, with their limestone walls, hanging roots and pooled light, make a cinematic counterpoint to the open beach. We know access and the quiet hours, and can pair a cenote with a Playacar beach in a single half-day if you want both moods.

What time of day do you photograph?

We build the session around golden hour, the last seventy-five to ninety minutes before sunset, roughly 5:00 to 7:00 PM depending on the month, when the beach turns warm and thins out. The east-facing Playa del Carmen sand is also beautiful and nearly empty at sunrise for couples who prefer an early, private start.

Can the session pair with a wedding collection?

Yes. Many couples book a Playa del Carmen couples or engagement session alongside a destination wedding collection, so one studio holds the whole story in a single visual language. The session also doubles as a relaxed rehearsal in front of the camera. You can book it standalone or as part of a wedding.

How do we book?

Send your dates, where you are staying in or near Playa del Carmen, and the locations you are drawn to. We reply the same business day with availability and a planning conversation, then send a proposal with the session quoted by inquiry. A retainer and signed agreement secure your date.
