Maternity · Babymoon

The hour before the trip ends and the chapter begins.

Maternity sessions for the babymoon. Late golden hour, soft direction, restrained styling. Riviera Maya, Cancún, Los Cabos.

Send me your travel dates WhatsApp

The session, considered

What we plan. What we deliver.

Maternity sessions are paced gently. Comfort comes before the shot list — and the shot list still gets done.

01

What we plan

  • The light window. We schedule a late golden-hour slot — the last warm hour before sunset, when the air softens and the horizon does the work.
  • A comfort-first location. Flat sand, shaded paths to and from, a private bench within reach. No long walks, no slippery rock, no rushed transitions.
  • Outfit guide, body-friendly. Skewed to flowing fabrics, body-skimming neutrals from the brand palette. Specific note: nothing tight at the waist. Linen, silk, soft jersey — they read as movement, not constriction.
  • Wind contingency. A backup interior or open-shade alternative is held the morning of. Coastal wind is part of the look — until it isn't, and then we move.
  • Partner inclusion, your call. Solo portraits, partner-included frames, or both within the same window. The direction adapts; you don't.

02

What we deliver

  • A 90-minute window. Long enough to settle into the light, short enough that nothing is asked of you twice.
  • A color-graded, soft-warm gallery. Hand-culled. Skin tones warm and natural, shadows held in the IVAE register — not flat, not overworked.
  • One to three days delivery. Private, password-protected gallery. Print and web sizes included. The full set arrives before the trip ends.

The right window

Most sessions land between weeks twenty-eight and thirty-four.

The bump has shape, the energy is still there, the trip feels reachable. That is the editorial sweet spot — and it is also the reason most of our maternity calendar fills inside that window.

We can do earlier, and we can do later. The window comes from comfort and bump shape, not from rules. If you are travelling for the babymoon and the dates already exist, we work with them — light is the only non-negotiable.

One quiet note: clear travel and photography with your physician before booking. We follow your lead on movement, length and pace.

Where we shoot maternity

Three coastlines. One direction.

Beach, resort interior and open-shade options across the three regions. Cenotes are off-limits for maternity sessions — cold water, slippery surfaces, uneven footing. We don't book them, and we don't recommend them.

Common questions

What expecting parents ask first.

Four short answers. If yours isn't here, write directly — we respond within 24 hours.

When is the right time to schedule?

Most maternity sessions land between weeks 28 and 34. The bump has shape, the energy is still there, and travel is still comfortable. Earlier or later is possible — the window comes from comfort and bump shape, not from rules. Send your travel dates and we'll align the session to a late golden-hour slot during your stay.

What should I wear?

Flowing fabrics, body-skimming neutrals from the brand palette — warm sand, resort white, soft slate, atlantic navy. Linen, silk and soft jersey photograph as movement; nothing tight at the waist. If your partner is in the frame, we coordinate them in tonal neutrals so the bump reads first. A one-page outfit guide is sent once the session is confirmed.

Will my partner be in the photos?

Yes, if you want. We direct both — solo portraits, partner-included frames, or a mix. Most couples prefer to keep both. The 90-minute window is paced so neither of you feels asked to hold a pose; the direction is quiet and conversational.

How much does it cost?

Investment varies by length, location and resort. Maternity sessions are quoted per-trip rather than from a tiered list, because the right window depends on your dates and gestational stage. Send your travel dates and resort, and we respond within 24 hours with availability and a detailed quote.
