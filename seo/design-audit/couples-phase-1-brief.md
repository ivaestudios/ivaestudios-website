## 1. Executive Summary

The current `/couples-photography.html` (758 lines) is a v1 service brochure. No manifesto, no pull-quote, no drop cap, no cinemascope, no drag-scroll reel, no widget, no count-up, no SVG ornament. The wedding page is the cinematic peer at 12 wow features deployed; the couples page is two design generations behind. This redesign is peer-elevation: couples must look like a service of the same studio, not a smaller cousin. Conversion math differs from weddings and family. A couples session is $850 to $2,500 with three lead-time profiles: 1 to 2 weeks for proposal, 4 to 8 weeks for honeymoon, 6 to 12 weeks for anniversary. The page must serve all three without three landing pages, single hero, three pillars, three tiers, and one widget that helps every persona pick golden hour around their itinerary. Studio voice inherits the wedding register (third-person, calm, editorial) but accommodates a wider emotional range from discreet proposal logistics to languid 15th-anniversary pacing. Twelve cinematic features are recommended for Phase 3, three couples-native: heart and infinity SVG ornament, proposal countdown widget, sunset-clock for proposals and golden hour. Gender-inclusive throughout, some couples are same-sex.

## 3. Top 3 Critiques of Current Page

**Critique 1. No editorial weight, no signature feature.** The current page (lines 78 to 695) reads as a flat service brochure. Hero is 80vh with one h1, one subhead, one CTA, no availability widget, no count-up, no ornament, no motes, no grain (lines 78 to 92). After hero comes a stats strip, a why-section with a single image, a 4-card gallery grid, a 3-card destinations block, a 4-card sessions list, a 6-step journey, three testimonial cards, a FAQ, internal links, a CTA. Twelve sections of comparable visual weight, no peak. The wedding page solves this with a sticky-stage manifesto, a 21:9 cinemascope feature, a giant Cormorant pull-quote, and an interactive sunset-clock widget. The couples page has none of these. A reader cannot tell which frame the studio considers its anchor image, which client quote it considers definitive, or which moment it considers signature.

**Critique 2. Voice and CTA are too transactional for the emotional register.** Header CTA reads "Book Now" (line 142), hero CTA reads "Book Your Session" (line 168), inquiry CTA repeats "Book Your Session" (line 673). For a $1,500 to $2,500 anniversary trip booking after months of planning, "Book Now" is the wrong register. The wedding page uses "Begin Inquiry" which respects the deliberation. The current page also writes in first-person plural ("our team is ready," line 671), which is a brochure register, not an editorial-studio register. Worst of all, the CTA section subhead at line 671 reads "Whether it's a honeymoon, anniversary, proposal, or simply a romantic getaway worth remembering" which is a grocery-list conjunction that asks the reader to self-select before the studio has earned trust. Studio voice should name the moment for the reader.

**Critique 3. Persona-blindness in the IA.** The same page serves three completely different jobs to be done (proposal logistics, honeymoon languor, anniversary milestone) without acknowledging that the three readers want different proof. A proposal client needs discreet-coverage logistics, restaurant coordination, and a "next available proposal date" availability proof. A honeymoon couple needs golden-hour reassurance, post-wedding-glow framing, and packing-light wardrobe guidance. An anniversary couple needs milestone narrative, "returning to the place where" pull-quotes, and longer-form coverage tiers. The current page treats all three as one "Couples & Honeymoon" reader and gives all three the same gallery, the same 4 session cards, the same FAQ. The Phase 4 IA must keep one page but introduce a soft-segmentation pattern (three tier cards named for the three jobs, three pillar headlines that each persona maps onto, one widget that threads all three).

## 4. The Three Personas

### Persona A. Proposal / Engagement: The Surprise Proposal Traveler

**Name:** Daniel and Future-Spouse. Daniel is the planner; the partner being proposed to is unnamed by design.
**Composition:** 1 partner planning in secret, 1 unaware. Often male-planning-for-female, but increasingly same-sex (about 1 in 5).
**Origin:** Chicago, Toronto, Boston, Miami, Mexico City, Madrid, London. East Coast US for Cancun, West Coast for Cabo.
**Resort tier:** Nizuc, Rosewood Mayakoba, Banyan Tree, Le Blanc, One&Only Palmilla. $600 to $1,400 per night.
**Booking lead time:** 1 to 2 weeks. Often 4 to 7 days. Sometimes 48 hours.
**Tier chosen:** The Hour or The Sunset ($850 to $1,500). Discreet first-coverage of the proposal, then a 30 to 45 minute portrait session immediately after.
**Job to be done:** "I want my partner to remember the moment they said yes the way they actually felt it. A photographer hidden, our first portraits as an engaged couple within an hour of the yes."
**Primary fear:** the photographer will be obvious and ruin the surprise, the partner will be too overwhelmed to pose, or the restaurant or beach will say no to a tripod.
**What they need from the page:** a "next available proposal date" availability proof, a clear discreet-coverage workflow, a restaurant coordination FAQ, a sample frame of an actual proposal candid.
**Voice they respond to:** logistical-first, emotional-second. "We arrive ninety minutes before you. We brief the restaurant. We sit at the table you cannot see. We do not take a single frame until you kneel." Operational confidence over romantic prose.
**Page elements that convert:** the proposal countdown widget, the discreet-coverage step in Method, the restaurant coordination FAQ, the heart-and-infinity SVG ornament.

### Persona B. Honeymoon Couple: The Newlywed Glow

**Name:** Ana and Marco
**Composition:** 2 people, recently married (within 2 to 6 months of the wedding). Ages 25 to 38. Sometimes the wedding was in Mexico, sometimes abroad.
**Origin:** New York, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Toronto, Mexico City, Madrid, Bogota, London. Heavy US Coasts plus growing LATAM segment.
**Resort tier:** Mayakoba properties, Nizuc, Excellence Playa Mujeres, Maroma Belmond, Esperanza Cabo, Waldorf Pedregal. $450 to $1,000 per night, often via credit-card travel points.
**Booking lead time:** 4 to 8 weeks. Same headspace as the wedding photographer they recently hired.
**Tier chosen:** The Sunset ($1,200 to $1,500), occasionally The Adventure Day ($1,800 to $2,500) for cenote plus beach plus resort coverage.
**Job to be done:** "We just got married, we still have the dress and the suit, we want one set of frames that captures us in this post-wedding glow before everyday life resumes. One frame for the bedroom wall. Twenty for Instagram."
**Primary fear:** the post-wedding exhaustion will show, the photos will feel like a do-over of the wedding photos, or rain will spoil the one window.
**What they need from the page:** a sample frame of post-wedding-glow couples, a wardrobe guidance block, a rain-reschedule promise, clear honeymoon-versus-engagement positioning.
**Voice they respond to:** warm, languid, references to "rest," "second wind," "the glow," "the first proper morning together," "the dress one more time." Warmer than the proposal persona, more emotional, more present-tense.
**Page elements that convert:** the cinemascope feature on a honeymoon couple, the wardrobe color palette by month, the pull-quote, the Adventure Day tier, the newlywed testimonial.

### Persona C. Anniversary Couple: The Milestone Return

**Name:** Sarah and Michael
**Composition:** 2 people, 35 to 55 years old, celebrating 5th, 10th, 15th, 20th, or 25th anniversary. Kids stayed home or are old enough for resort kids-club.
**Origin:** Chicago, Atlanta, Seattle, Boston, Toronto, Vancouver, Mexico City, Bogota, Madrid. Higher-income, deeper deliberation, stronger brand sense.
**Resort tier:** Esperanza, Las Ventanas, Rosewood Mayakoba, Maroma Belmond, Four Seasons Costa Palmas, One&Only Palmilla, Le Blanc Adults Only. $700 to $1,500 per night.
**Booking lead time:** 6 to 12 weeks, often 3+ months for milestones (10th, 25th).
**Tier chosen:** The Sunset ($1,500) or The Adventure Day ($2,000 to $3,000). Long-form coverage with a destination shift (resort, cenote, sunset on a yacht).
**Job to be done:** "We came back because we honeymooned here twelve years ago. I want frames where we look like the couple we are now, not the couple we were then. One frame for the foyer that tells the story of where we have been."
**Primary fear:** the photos will look like brochure stock, they will look stiff because they are not 25 anymore, or the photographer will pose them like a wedding.
**What they need from the page:** evidence of editorial direction with couples in their 40s and 50s, a manifesto line that names "returning" or "milestone," sample frames of post-honeymoon couples, a high-end tier with cenote or yacht segment.
**Voice they respond to:** restrained, observational, slightly literary. "The studio listens for the moment one of you laughs at the other. That is the frame." References to "the years between."
**Page elements that convert:** the pull-quote on "the years between," the cinemascope feature, the milestone testimonial, the Adventure Day tier with cenote inclusion, the FAQ on multi-location pacing.

## 7. Five Voice Rules

1. **Third-person studio voice, by default, not always.** "The studio plans," "the team works," "the hour is built around the two of you" as the dominant register. First-person plural ("we") is permitted for FAQ answers (where it matches conversational expectation) and for the inquiry block (where it warms the close). Avoid first-person plural in hero, manifesto, pillars, and pull-quote. Never use "I." The studio is plural by name and by voice.

2. **Zero em-dashes.** The brief and the page body use periods, commas, parentheses, or the visible " / " divider. Em-dashes signal hurried prose and the page's editorial register depends on the breath that punctuation provides. The wedding and family pages already enforce this rule, the couples page must match.

3. **Gender-inclusive throughout.** Some couples are same-sex. Copy never says "bride and groom," "her dress and his suit," "she said yes." Use "the two of you," "the partner being proposed to," "the dress or the suit," "the moment of the yes," "your partner." The FAQ explicitly names same-sex couples once: "We photograph all couples. Same-sex weddings, proposals, and anniversaries are part of our regular practice." This single sentence is the page's inclusion proof.

4. **Vianey is "Director" (EN) or "Directora" (ES, feminine).** Never "the photographer" alone, never "Vianey shoots," never first-person "I." When she appears in copy at all, she appears in the bio block or testimonial signoff as "Director Vianey Diaz" / "Directora Vianey Diaz." The studio is the subject of every operational sentence.

5. **Restraint over emotion. One ornament per moment.** One drop cap per page. One pull-quote per page. One cinemascope per page. The proposal persona reads operational; the anniversary persona reads literary; the honeymoon persona reads warm. The voice never combines all three at once. A sentence is either calm or warm or literary, never all three. The page rotates registers across sections, not within sentences.

## 8. Twelve Cinematic Features

Three are couples-native (marked ★). Nine adapt the wedding-side wow features to couples imagery.

1. **3D mouse parallax hero.** Same technique as wedding (`perspective: 1200px` on `.lc-hero` parent, transform on `.lc-hero-img` based on mouse delta). Disabled on `pointer:coarse` and `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.

2. **SVG film grain.** `--lc-grain-opacity: 0.030`, slightly lighter than wedding's 0.035. `aria-hidden`, `pointer-events: none`, gated on `prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference`.

3. **Floating gold motes.** 8 motes drifting upward at 28s loop. Pure ornament, `aria-hidden`.

4. **Magnetic CTAs.** `data-magnet` attribute on hero and inquiry CTAs. Shimmer sweep on hover. Underline plus color contrast hold without motion.

5. **Heart and infinity SVG ornament ★.** Couples-native. Beside each tier card: The Hour shows a single heart outline, The Sunset shows two interlocked hearts forming a vesica, The Adventure Day shows a horizontal infinity glyph. Stroke draws over 1.4s on tier-card hover or focus. Decorative role.

6. **"500+ couples since 2019" count-up.** Same `data-count-to` pattern as wedding. Counts 0 to 500 over 1.6s on first scroll into view. `aria-label` on parent carries final number.

7. **Sticky-stage manifesto.** Pillars section uses `position: sticky` for stage column on `min-width: 1024px`. Three pillars (Light, Direction, Discretion) scroll past while manifesto headline plus lede plus pull-quote stay anchored. Disabled on tablet/mobile.

8. **Drag-scroll romantic moments reel ★.** Couples-native variant. Six frames at 4:5, captions name the *moment* (the kneel, the morning of the dress, the second sunset, the year fifteen, the cenote dive, the laugh). Scroll-snap mandatory on touch, arrow-key for keyboard.

9. **Sunset clock + proposal countdown widget ★.** Couples-native. SVG clock face like wedding's plus a small countdown card showing "Next available proposal date." Twelve month buttons update the SVG and recommend a start time. Proposal countdown fed by static JSON of open Saturdays.

10. **Cinemascope on featured couple.** Section 5. 21:9 letterbox (`aspect-ratio: 21/9` with `--lc-letterbox: 8%` ink-dark bars + gold hairline). Featured image: anniversary couple at Cabo Arch sunset. Caption with drop cap. Meta cells: Coast / Years Together / Coverage.

11. **Drop cap on featured caption.** `::first-letter` selector. Cormorant Garamond italic 300, `--lc-dropcap-size: clamp(58px, 6.5vw, 78px)`. Used once on the page.

12. **Pull-quote with massive Cormorant `❝`.** Section 7. Giant `❝` at `clamp(180px, 22vw, 320px)` at 0.18 opacity. Quote in Cormorant 300 italic at clamp(22px, 2.6vw, 38px). The anniversary quote.

14. **Discreet-coverage visual diagram** for proposal persona. A small SVG inside Method showing the proposal-coverage workflow in five panels.

## 9. Three Visual Directions

### Direction A. Editorial Vogue
Vogue Living couples-feature spread. Generous margins (clamp(120px, 14vw, 200px) gutter on desktop), Cormorant Garamond serif headings, drop cap once, sans-serif only for eyebrows and stats. Cinemascope dominates as the magazine cover. Reel as contact sheet (3-column grid on desktop, drag-scroll only on mobile). Pull-quote uses giant `❝` at 0.18 opacity. No grain on hero. Motes capped at 4. Pillars sticky-stage active. Cream-1 dominant. Mood: Vogue Living "An Anniversary in Capri" December 2023.

### Direction B. Cinematic Film
Cinematic title sequence. Deep dark (ink-3 / ink-4) dominant. Hero 100vh with 3D parallax, grain 0.030, motes 8, letterboxing extended into hero. Cinemascope full-bleed 21:9 with deeper 10% bars. Sunset clock + proposal countdown gets dramatic treatment (320px SVG, gradient ring, cinematic shadow, countdown as film-reel subtitle card). Reel with frame numbers and hover preview. Pillars sticky-stage with scroll-driven word reveals. Pull-quote on dark with gold `❝` at 0.22 opacity. Mood: A24 title sequence + Loro Piana resort feature film.

### Direction C. Minimalist Refined (RECOMMENDED)
Hermes / Loro Piana / The Row. Edge-flush whitespace, no ornaments except one. Two CTAs total (hero primary + inquiry primary). Reel is a single-row 3-column grid, no drag. Cinemascope is the only decorative moment. No grain, no motes, no count-up. Proposal countdown is a single caption line, not a widget. Pillars in a single linear column, no sticky stage. Sunset clock reduced to a simple SVG pie + month buttons. Cream-1 dominant with one ink-3 break. Mood: Hermes silk scarf catalogue + The Row site.

**Recommendation:** Direction C. Couples serves three personas (proposal/honeymoon/anniversary), and Direction C's restraint is the only register all three accept. Editorial Vogue (A) is too bridal-magazine for the proposal planner. Cinematic Film (B) is too dramatic for the honeymoon couple. Minimalist Refined holds the room for everyone and lets the imagery do the emotional work.

## 10. Pricing Strategy

Three tiers, recommended names plus price ranges:

| # | Recommended name | Owner-readable description | Price | Length | Locations | Group size |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Hour** | One golden hour, one location. Couple portraits. Honeymoon or anniversary candid. | from $850 USD | 60 min | 1 | 2 |
| 2 | **The Sunset** *(Most Chosen)* | Two locations, dressy + relaxed. Proposal coverage available. | from $1,500 USD | 90 min | 2 | 2 |
| 3 | **The Adventure Day** | Three locations. Resort + beach + cenote or yacht. Half-day editorial. | from $2,500 USD | 4 hours | 3 | 2 |

**Rationale:**
- Editorial tier names mirror wedding (Essentials, Signature, Cinematic) and family (The Hour, The Afternoon, The Reunion). Couples-side names should be temporal and atmospheric (an hour, a sunset, an adventure) because couples buy *the moment*, not session-count.
- "*Most Chosen*" badge on tier 2 mirrors wedding pattern.
- Pricing range aligns with the three personas: The Hour serves proposal post-yes-portraits and budget honeymoons, The Sunset serves the typical honeymoon and standard anniversary, The Adventure Day serves milestone anniversaries and high-budget honeymoons.
- "from $XXX USD" leaves room for peak-season uplift.
- Three tiers, not four. A custom "Vow Renewal" or "Trash the Dress" tier would dilute. Custom inquiries route through the inquiry block.

**Alternative names considered and rejected:**
- "Honeymoon / Anniversary / Proposal" as tier names. Too persona-locked, forces the reader to self-select before the studio has earned trust.
- "Petite / Standard / Grand." Too restaurant-menu.
- "Half Hour / One Hour / Half Day." Too literal, no editorial register.
- "The Glow / The Promise / The Year." Too poetic, loses operational clarity.

**Pricing presentation rules:**
- Display "from $XXX USD" not "$XXX USD."
- Currency: USD primary, MXN secondary in inquiry follow-up only.
- No strikethroughs, no "originally $X."
- Tier cards order: tier 1 left, tier 2 (featured) center with `transform: scale(1.04)` and `--inv-tier-rule-featured` gold rule, tier 3 right.

## 11. WCAG 2.1 AA Accessibility Contract (12 Element-Level Rules)

1. **3D mouse parallax hero.** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, fall back to static. Disabled on `pointer: coarse`. (1.4.11, 2.3.3)
2. **Drag-scroll reel.** Arrow-key scrolling on focus, visible focus ring. `tabindex="0"`, `role="region"`, `aria-label="Romantic moments reel, six frames"`. (2.1.1, 2.4.7)
3. **Sunset clock + proposal countdown.** Month buttons `aria-pressed`, SVG `role="img"` with announcing label. Countdown has `aria-live="polite"`. (4.1.2, 4.1.3, 1.1.1)
4. **Magnetic CTAs.** Hover transform must not be the only affordance. Underline + color contrast hold without motion. Focus-visible ring `outline: 2px solid var(--gold); outline-offset: 4px`. (2.4.7, 2.4.11)
5. **Floating gold motes.** Decorative, `aria-hidden="true"`, gated on `prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference`. (1.1.1, 2.3.3)
6. **SVG film grain.** `aria-hidden="true"`, `pointer-events: none`, no animation in reduced-motion. (2.3.3)
7. **Heart and infinity SVG ornament.** Decorative for v6 (tier card text names the tier), `aria-hidden`. (4.1.2)
8. **Count-up stats.** Screen readers announce final value, not counting. `aria-label` on parent, counting digits `aria-hidden`. (4.1.2, 4.1.3)
9. **Pull-quote `❝` ornament.** `aria-hidden="true"`. `<blockquote>` wraps quote with `<cite>` for source. (1.3.1)
