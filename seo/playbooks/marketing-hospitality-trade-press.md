# IVAE Marketing: Hospitality and Marketing Trade Press Playbook

Pre-built pitches for the twenty publications where IVAE Marketing should
land thought-leadership features in the next twelve months. Built for Vianey
to send. Every pitch is editorial, named, specific. No press release filler.
No "freeze the moment" language. No em-dashes in any body copy.

Two strategic objectives:

1. **Hospitality trade press** (10 publications): the publications hotel
   general managers and marketing directors actually read. These features
   convert into client inquiries directly.

2. **Marketing trade press** (5 publications): the publications agency
   founders, in-house marketing leads, and brand strategists read. These
   features convert into agency-of-record consideration and high-DA backlinks.

3. **Hospitality blogs and podcasts in Mexico** (5 outlets): the regional
   press that builds local credibility with Cancún and Riviera Maya operators
   the agency wants on its client roster.

Editor email addresses are research-confirmed where possible and
intelligence-estimated where the editor's direct line is gated. Use the
research estimate as a starting point and validate with the publication's
contact page or LinkedIn before sending.

# Universal pitching rules

1. **One pitch per publication per cycle**. Never blast multiple pitches to
   the same editor in the same month. The editor remembers.

2. **Send on Tuesday or Wednesday before 10 a.m. local time of the publication's
   headquarters**. Monday is overload, Thursday is wind-down, Friday is dead.

3. **Subject line in 60 characters or under**. Editors scan inboxes from
   their phones.

4. **No attachments in the first pitch**. Attachments trigger spam filters.
   Include a portfolio URL or share a Google Drive link in the body if
   needed.

5. **Always include a draft delivery window in the pitch body** (typically
   "within ten days of approval"). The editor needs to know you can deliver
   on their calendar.

6. **Zero em-dashes in any pitch body, in English or Spanish**. The voice
   contract holds across every publication.

7. **Track everything in a single spreadsheet**: publication, editor, send
   date, response, status, follow-up date, published URL. Without the
   spreadsheet, follow-ups slip.

8. **Send the pitch from the founder's address (info@ivaestudios.com), not
   a generic press@ address**. Founder pitches respond at a materially higher
   rate than agency-PR-team pitches.

9. **No press release filler**. Never use the phrases "award-winning",
   "leading agency", "industry-leading", "premier", "captures the moment",
   "freezes time". These phrases are auto-rejected at the editorial level.

10. **The follow-up is half the work**. A pitch with no follow-up has a
    20 percent response rate. A pitch with two follow-ups has a 60 percent
    response rate. The follow-up is not optional.
