# IVAE Marketing: Competitive Analysis: Mexico SMM Agencies

Honest competitive intelligence on the five social media agencies in Mexico
that compete most directly with IVAE Marketing for luxury hospitality clients.
Built for internal use by Vianey, the marketing director, and any new
business team member. The goal is to know where IVAE Marketing wins, where
the competition wins, and which moves close the gap in the next ninety days.

This is not a marketing document. It is a strategic one. No competitor is
slandered; every weakness is documented because it is observable in their
public output. Every IVAE Marketing weakness is named here too, because
pretending the gap does not exist is the fastest way to keep losing on it.

Geographic focus: Cancún, Riviera Maya, Tulum, Los Cabos, Ciudad de México.
Vertical focus: luxury hospitality (boutique hotels, resort brands, fine-dining
restaurants, wellness retreats).

Authority and traffic numbers are estimates synthesized from public Ahrefs,
SEMrush and SimilarWeb snapshots as of May 2026. Treat as directional, not
precise.

---

# Competitor 1: Sociallyin (Latin America office)

**Website**: https://sociallyin.com
**Mexico office**: Sociallyin LATAM, Cd. de México
**Founded**: 2011 (parent), 2019 (Mexico expansion)
**Domain authority (Ahrefs DR)**: 58
**Estimated referring domains**: 1,420
**Estimated monthly organic traffic (LATAM-language pages)**: 18,000 visits
**Headcount**: 80-plus globally, approximately 12 in Mexico City

### Service offering breakdown
Full-stack social media agency. Social strategy, content production (in-house
photo and video team), community management, paid social, influencer
relations, social listening, monthly reporting. Hospitality is one of nine
verticals they list (alongside SaaS, retail, healthcare, etc.).

### Pricing transparency
Opaque. No published rates. Pitch process gates pricing. Sales cycle typically
6-8 weeks from first inquiry to signed retainer. Industry chatter places their
hospitality retainers in the $8K-$25K USD per month range.

### Client roster (public references)
The Mexico office has named Grupo Posadas (Fiesta Americana, Live Aqua),
Hyatt Hotels Corporation (Mexico region), Vivanta by Taj (single property),
and a few enterprise non-hospitality accounts. Most case studies on their site
are US-based.

### Content quality assessment (Instagram audit, @sociallyin_)
Account is the agency's own brand showcase rather than client work. Mix of
behind-the-scenes office content, recruiting posts, and industry commentary.
Production polish is high. Editorial register reads "marketing agency"
not "luxury hospitality content studio".

The work they have done for Mexican hotel clients (visible on each property's
own Instagram, not Sociallyin's feed) skews toward stock-adjacent content
production. The hotel rooms look like hotel rooms. The food shots look like
food shots. There is technical competence and almost no editorial signature.

### Unique angle / differentiator
Scale. They can run integrated paid social, organic, influencer and analytics
under one roof for enterprise clients. They have data scientists. They can
absorb a hotel chain.

### Where IVAE Marketing beats them
- Editorial photography as native service vs. retrofitted content production
- Founder-led account direction vs. account-executive layer between client and creative
- Bilingual fluency at every position (Sociallyin Mexico team is bilingual at the AE layer; creative work often runs through US team in English first, translated second)
- Speed: IVAE delivers monthly editorial sessions in 7 days; Sociallyin's content pipeline runs 3-4 weeks
- Pricing accessibility: IVAE Essential at $1,800/mo serves the boutique hotel that Sociallyin will not take below $8K
- Hospitality-only focus vs. nine-vertical generalism

### Where Sociallyin beats IVAE Marketing
- Paid social capability (IVAE does not currently offer media buying as a primary service)
- Influencer relations at scale (Sociallyin has dedicated influencer roster)
- Analytics depth (data science team; IVAE reports are editorial-quality but lighter on attribution modeling)
- Enterprise contract handling, MSAs, procurement compliance
- US-based account team for US-headquartered hotel groups whose marketing decisions happen in Dallas, Miami or New York rather than Mexico City

### Honest gap to close
IVAE Marketing cannot compete with Sociallyin for a Marriott or IHG corporate
account. IVAE should not try. The play is to win the independent boutique
hotel that Sociallyin will not return calls from, and the F&B brand inside the
Marriott property that the corporate agency is not covering.

# Competitor 2: Be a Lion (Cancún)

**Website**: https://bealion.mx
**Mexico office**: Cancún (headquarters)
**Founded**: 2016
**Domain authority (Ahrefs DR)**: 32
**Estimated referring domains**: 180
**Estimated monthly organic traffic**: 2,400 visits
**Headcount**: 8-10

### Service offering breakdown
Local Cancún social media agency. Social media management, basic photo and
video, paid ads (Meta and Google), web design, branding. They position as a
"360 marketing partner" rather than a content specialist. Hospitality is one
of four named industries.

### Pricing transparency
Semi-transparent. Their website lists three monthly tiers: Starter at MXN
$8,000 (approximately $470 USD), Growth at MXN $18,000 (approximately $1,050
USD), and Premium at MXN $35,000 (approximately $2,050 USD). Pricing is at
the lower end of the local market.

### Client roster
A mix of mid-tier Cancún hotels (none in the boutique luxury segment that IVAE
Marketing targets), a few restaurants, real estate developments, and dental
clinics. The hospitality clients are 3-star to 4-star, not the 5-star
boutique market.

### Content quality assessment (Instagram audit, @bealion.mx)
The agency's own Instagram is competent for a local Cancún SMM. The work they
deliver to hotel clients is recognizably stock-aided, with frequent use of
Canva templates and recycled hero shots between months. Photography is
serviceable, never editorial. The food and drink content uses obvious filter
presets. The grid lacks a distinct visual signature; you could swap any of
their hotel client feeds and the buyer would not notice.

This is the kind of work IVAE Marketing was built to compete against and out-
produce.

### Unique angle / differentiator
Price accessibility for the small Cancún hotel and restaurant market. Local
presence, Spanish-first communication, account managers physically in Cancún
who can attend client meetings.

### Where IVAE Marketing beats them
- Visual quality on every dimension that matters: photography, editing, copy register, brand consistency
- Hospitality vertical specialization vs. generalist Cancún agency
- Bilingual native at every position vs. Spanish-primary team
- Founder is a working creative director, not a sales executive
- The boutique hotel segment ($350+ ADR properties) does not consider Be a Lion as a serious option

### Where Be a Lion beats IVAE Marketing
- Pricing accessibility for hotels under 50 keys with a sub-$200 ADR
- Paid media as a packaged service (IVAE adds this on Signature tier only)
- Web design and full brand identity work
- Physical proximity to non-luxury Cancún hotel zone properties
- Spanish-only communication for clients who prefer Spanish-primary teams

### Honest gap to close
Be a Lion competes for the segment below IVAE Marketing's positioning. They
are not a direct threat in the boutique luxury market. The competitive risk
is reputational: in casual conversation among hotel managers, "Be a Lion" and
"IVAE Marketing" can both be referred to as "the local SMM" even though the
work product is two distinct tiers. The brand differentiation work is on
IVAE Marketing's side.

# Competitor 3: La Familia (Mexico City)

**Website**: https://lafamilia.mx
**Mexico office**: Polanco, Ciudad de México
**Founded**: 2014
**Domain authority (Ahrefs DR)**: 42
**Estimated referring domains**: 320
**Estimated monthly organic traffic**: 7,800 visits
**Headcount**: 25-30

### Service offering breakdown
Boutique creative agency. Branding, design, social media, content production,
campaign work. They publicly position as "an agency for brands that want to
mean something". The hospitality work sits inside a broader brand-building
practice that includes consumer goods, fashion, and lifestyle clients.

### Pricing transparency
Opaque. No published rates. Their reputation places retainers in the
$5K-$15K USD per month range. They reportedly turn down accounts below $6K.

### Client roster
Strong roster of fashion, lifestyle and beverage brands. Hospitality work
includes Grupo Habita (Hotel Habita, Condesa DF, Distrito Capital), Casa
Bosques in Polanco, and a handful of restaurants in the Roma-Condesa corridor.
They have not visibly worked with Cancún or Riviera Maya properties.

### Content quality assessment (Instagram audit, @lafamilia.mx)
The strongest creative competitor on this list. La Familia's work shows a
coherent editorial voice, considered photography (often shot by named
photographers credited on the post), and copywriting that holds register. The
hotel client feeds (notably Grupo Habita) are among the best hotel feeds in
Mexico.

The agency operates at a fashion-and-lifestyle creative bar, not a hotel-
specific one. Their hospitality clients are also the most editorially mature
hotel brands in Mexico to begin with.

### Unique angle / differentiator
Creative reputation. They are the agency clients reference when they want a
"considered" brand presence. Strong network in Mexican fashion, art and
design press. They sit inside the cultural conversation that boutique
hospitality wants to be part of.

### Where IVAE Marketing beats them
- Hospitality vertical specialization with operator-aware deliverables (revenue attribution, season-aware calendars, OTA-aware copy)
- Cancún and Riviera Maya geographic native presence vs. Mexico City based with project-trip production
- Photography native to the service vs. contracting photographers per project
- Lower price entry point ($1,800 Essential vs. their reported $6K floor)
- Bilingual native vs. Spanish-primary with English on request
- Speed of delivery: monthly editorial cadence is faster than La Familia's campaign-cycle approach

### Where La Familia beats IVAE Marketing
- Cultural authority in Mexican design and lifestyle press
- Fashion and consumer brand crossover capability
- Mexico City client roster IVAE Marketing has not entered
- Brand identity and design work alongside social
- Press distribution: La Familia gets clients into Vogue MX, Travesías, GQ
- Length of track record (10+ years)

### Honest gap to close
La Familia is the creative competitor IVAE Marketing should study most
carefully. Their visual register and brand discipline are both high. The
beat is to specialize harder into hospitality operator economics (which La
Familia treats as one of several practice areas) and to win the Cancún/Riviera
Maya market that La Familia is geographically distant from.

The gap to close on IVAE Marketing's side: cultural press placements (Travesías,
Forbes Life, Travel + Leisure Mexico). IVAE Marketing has earned editorial
features for the photography studio but not yet for the marketing division.

# Competitor 4: Mantequilla (Mexico City)

**Website**: https://mantequilla.mx
**Mexico office**: Roma Norte, Ciudad de México
**Founded**: 2018
**Domain authority (Ahrefs DR)**: 38
**Estimated referring domains**: 240
**Estimated monthly organic traffic**: 4,100 visits
**Headcount**: 15-20

### Service offering breakdown
Social media and content agency with strong focus on F&B and hospitality.
Content production (photo and video), social media management, community,
influencer activations, micro-campaigns. They explicitly position as "the
F&B agency" in Mexico City.

### Pricing transparency
Opaque. Some publicly available case study collateral references "monthly
investment from MXN $40,000" (approximately $2,350 USD), but this is
positioning rather than a published rate card.

### Client roster
Strong Mexico City F&B roster: Pujol's adjacent restaurants, Loup Bar, Niddo,
Maximo Bistrot, Rosetta extensions, a few coffee shops. Hospitality clients
include Casa Polanco and Pug Seal hotel group. They have not visibly worked
with Cancún or Riviera Maya properties.

### Content quality assessment (Instagram audit, @mantequillaco)
High-quality F&B content. They have a recognizable visual language: warm
tabletop lighting, considered prop styling, hand-held video that feels like a
chef's pass rather than a TikTok edit. Their hotel work is lighter than their
restaurant work, which is the strongest in their portfolio.

### Unique angle / differentiator
F&B specialization. They are the agency Mexico City chefs name when asked
"who do I hire for my opening". Restaurant operator-aware. Strong influencer
network in CDMX food media.

### Where IVAE Marketing beats them
- Hotel-and-resort specialization (Mantequilla is F&B-primary)
- Cancún and Riviera Maya geographic presence
- Photography native rather than contracted
- Bilingual native vs. Spanish-primary
- International guest audience knowledge (IVAE clients are 60 percent US/Canada/EU guests; Mantequilla's clients are 80 percent local Mexico City market)
- Hotel ADR and RevPAR fluency for reporting

### Where Mantequilla beats IVAE Marketing
- F&B vertical depth (chef relationships, menu launch experience, restaurant week activations)
- Mexico City press relationships (Time Out, Chilango, Eater)
- Restaurant operator network IVAE Marketing has not entered
- Specific micro-trend awareness (CDMX coffee scene, mezcalerías, neo-bistronomy) that Cancún does not require

### Honest gap to close
Mantequilla is not a direct hospitality threat outside Mexico City, but they
are the competitor IVAE Marketing should watch if expanding into the CDMX
restaurant scene. The honest play: IVAE Marketing's hotel-restaurant package
(where the hotel's F&B outlet is managed together with the room product) is
a stronger offering than Mantequilla's restaurant-only practice. That is the
positioning wedge.

# Competitor 5: Carbono (Riviera Maya)

**Website**: https://carbono.mx
**Mexico office**: Playa del Carmen
**Founded**: 2017
**Domain authority (Ahrefs DR)**: 28
**Estimated referring domains**: 110
**Estimated monthly organic traffic**: 1,800 visits
**Headcount**: 6-8

### Service offering breakdown
Local Riviera Maya creative shop. Social media, content production,
photography, branding, web design. Broad mix of clients across hospitality,
real estate, and lifestyle.

### Pricing transparency
Semi-transparent. Their service deck mentions monthly retainers from MXN
$25,000 (approximately $1,470 USD). Pricing at the low end of the market.

### Client roster
A mix of mid-tier Riviera Maya hotels, several beach clubs (notably one in
Playa del Carmen and one in Tulum), real estate developers (mostly
Hyperborean and Veleta projects), and a few restaurants. None at the
boutique-luxury segment IVAE Marketing targets.

### Content quality assessment (Instagram audit, @carbono.creativos)
Inconsistent. The agency's own feed shows talent (one or two strong photo
sets) alongside lower-quality template carousels. The client work mirrors
this: when they have time to shoot original content the result is decent,
when the calendar pushes they fall back to stock-aided and template content.

The honest read: a talented small team without the editorial discipline or
operational consistency to compete at the IVAE Marketing tier.

### Unique angle / differentiator
Local Riviera Maya presence with creative ambition. Lower price point. Spanish-
first communication for clients who prefer it.

### Where IVAE Marketing beats them
- Visual quality consistency month after month
- Editorial register (Carbono's work is creative; IVAE Marketing's is editorial, which is a different bar)
- Operational reliability (IVAE Marketing's three-month engagement minimum and capped client load enforce consistency)
- Bilingual native team
- Founder track record (Vianey's name carries weight in the hotel manager community)
- Reporting depth

### Where Carbono beats IVAE Marketing
- Pricing entry point for hotels under $1,500 USD per month
- Real estate vertical work (Hyperborean, Veleta) IVAE Marketing has not entered
- Riviera Maya local presence (Playa del Carmen office vs. Cancún office)

### Honest gap to close
Carbono is a watchlist competitor more than a direct one. The risk is they
upgrade their operational discipline and start competing for $3K-$5K
retainers. The defensive move: IVAE Marketing's Editorial tier ($4,500 USD)
must be visibly superior in every public client feed Carbono can be compared
against. Visible work is the moat.

# Strategic recommendations: five moves for the next 90 days

The competitive position is strong on creative output and weak on press
distribution, paid media capability, and Mexico City client penetration.
The following five moves close the most important gaps without diluting the
position.

### Move 1: Pitch a Travesías editorial feature in the next 30 days

La Familia owns Travesías. Mantequilla owns Time Out and Chilango. IVAE
Marketing currently owns no Mexican luxury press for the marketing division.
The single fastest move to close the press gap is one Travesías feature on
the IVAE Marketing positioning ("How a Cancún editorial studio is rewriting
the hotel Instagram playbook").

Travesías editorial calendar is monthly. Pitch lands the next issue cycle if
sent within 30 days. Pitch template lives in
`/seo/playbooks/marketing-hospitality-trade-press.md`.

### Move 2: Publish three named case studies on the IVAE Marketing site in the next 60 days

The site currently has zero published case studies for the marketing
division. Competitors all publish case studies. Three full case studies (the
Cancún boutique hotel, the Mayakoba restaurant, the Tulum wellness retreat),
each at 1,200 to 1,800 words, each with before/after Instagram grid screenshots
and signed client quote permission, will close the credibility gap.

Each case study becomes a URL Google can rank, a backlink source for
directories, and a sales asset. Three case studies, three deliverables, sixty
days.

### Move 3: Launch a paid social capability as Signature-tier add-on by day 60

Sociallyin and La Familia both offer integrated paid media. IVAE Marketing
currently does not. The right play is not to build an internal paid team
(that takes 12+ months) but to white-label a paid social specialist as a
Signature-tier add-on. One certified Meta and TikTok ads operator on a fixed
monthly retainer to IVAE Marketing, billed through to clients at a markup.

Day 30: identify and trial three white-label paid partners.
Day 45: select one.
Day 60: launch the Signature tier with the paid media add-on visible on the
website pricing page.

This closes the most-named-by-buyers gap in IVAE Marketing's offering.

### Move 4: Enter Mexico City with a single anchor restaurant client by day 90

Mantequilla and La Familia own Mexico City. IVAE Marketing currently has no
CDMX presence. The first CDMX client does not need to be a hotel; it can be
a single restaurant that the founder has a relationship with. The strategic
purpose is geographic credibility, not revenue.

The first CDMX restaurant client unlocks:
- The CDMX content production capability (Vianey traveling once per month is
  sustainable up to two CDMX accounts)
- The Mexico City sub-page on the IVAE Marketing site
- The first Travesías co-feature (the agency and the client featured together)
- The Mexico City content sample for the next CDMX prospect

Day 90 target: one signed CDMX restaurant client at the Editorial tier
($4,500 USD per month).

### Move 5: Move all client feeds to a unified IVAE Marketing operating system by day 60

The single highest-leverage internal move. Right now, every client account
runs on the team's accumulated discipline rather than a documented system.
Sociallyin and La Familia both run on internal SOPs. As IVAE Marketing
scales from 4 to 8 accounts, the lack of a documented operating system will
become the bottleneck.

Document by day 60:
- Editorial calendar template (with seasonal hospitality triggers)
- On-property shoot brief template
- Monthly report template
- Community management response tree (English and Spanish)
- Client onboarding checklist
- Brand visual audit template

Each template becomes a deliverable to the client too. The same SOP that
makes the team faster also makes the agency look more professional than its
competitors. Two outcomes from one investment.

# What the competitive set does not yet know

IVAE Marketing has two assets the named competitors cannot replicate quickly.
The first is the parent studio's photography backlist (500-plus sessions of
editorial work across Cancún, Mayakoba, Tulum, Los Cabos venues). Every
photograph in that backlist is a frame that can ladder into a content series
for a marketing client whose property is in the same venue. The second is
Vianey's name. Hotel managers in Cancún and the Riviera Maya know Vianey from
the photography work. The marketing division benefits from a name that did
not have to be built from zero.

Neither asset shows up in a public competitive audit. Both are durable
advantages. The work of the next 90 days is to make them visible to buyers
who do not already know.
