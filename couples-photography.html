The session

Sixty to ninety minutes. Sunrise or sunset. The rest is composition.

A couples session at IVAE Studios is direction, not posing. We brief in advance — what you came here for, what you don't want to look like, what makes you laugh. We choose the location and the call-time around the light. Once the camera is up, we move you. Walking, leaning, breathing pauses, the back-and-forth of an actual conversation. Frames build from movement, never from a held smile.

The window is short on purpose. Golden hour gives you about seventy useful minutes of light per session — beyond that, color shifts and the gallery loses its register. We work the light hard, then we stop. You leave with a tight set, not a long one.

Bilingual direction — English and Spanish — runs the whole shoot, so neither of you has to translate or interpret. Children, dogs and one extra family member can be added inside the same window if briefed beforehand. Wardrobe is part of the booking: a short style note goes out with confirmation, tuned to your chosen location, palette and the wind that day.

Where

Three coasts. Three different tones of couples photograph.

We don't recommend a region by popularity. We recommend by the light it gives, the palette it leaves on skin, and the way it lets a couple move. Read the three below and we'll match you to the right one when you write.

  • Region · 01

    Riviera Maya

    Tulum, Akumal, Playa del Carmen. Coastal jungle and low private beaches, plus cenote sessions for couples who want something architectural. The most intimate of the three — narrow horizons, soft palettes, a slower pace.

  • Region · 02

    Cancún

    Hotel Zone, Playa Mujeres and Isla Mujeres. Open white sand, long Atlantic horizon, resort architecture as backdrop. Cinematic. Best for honeymoon couples staying at all-inclusive properties who want the photographs to read clean and bright.

  • Region · 03

    Los Cabos

    Pedregal, Lovers Beach, El Arco, the marina. Desert meets Pacific. Harder light, sharper graphic register, golden-hour tones unique to the Sea of Cortés. The most editorial-graphic of the three regions.

Common questions

What couples ask first.

Four short answers to the questions we hear most. If yours isn't here, write directly — we respond within 24 hours.

We feel awkward in front of cameras.

Most couples do. Editorial direction works around that — we don't ask for poses, we direct movement. By minute fifteen the camera is invisible. The session window is locked to seventy minutes specifically because the first ten minutes are warm-up; the strongest frames almost always sit between minute twenty and minute fifty, once the self-consciousness has burned off. You don't need to be photogenic. You need to be willing to walk and talk for an hour.

Best location for a couples session?

It depends on the register you want. Riviera Maya is intimate and editorial — coastal jungle, cenotes, low private beaches with a softer palette. Cancún reads cinematic — open white sand, the long Atlantic horizon, the resort architecture as a clean backdrop. Los Cabos is the most graphic of the three — desert meets Pacific, hard light reshaped by the Sea of Cortés. We recommend by tone, not by popularity. Send the resort, the dates, and a few photographs you respond to, and we'll write back with a fit.

Can we do a proposal session and not tell my partner?

Often. We coordinate with the client who's planning the proposal — timing, ring handoff, the moment of the question. We shoot from a distance with a longer lens for the first part, then come close for the celebration. We don't disclose to the receiving partner — that's part of the brief. We then continue as a regular couples session so you leave with a complete editorial set, not just the proposal frame. The whole thing reads as one event when delivered.

How long does it take?

Sixty to ninety minutes, scheduled inside the golden-hour window. Sunset call-time is roughly ninety minutes before sunset; sunrise sessions begin twenty minutes before first light. The session window is locked in writing before the deposit so wardrobe, transport and resort logistics can be planned around the light, not around our calendar. Anniversary couples sometimes book the full ninety; pre-engagement and proposal couples typically settle into seventy.
