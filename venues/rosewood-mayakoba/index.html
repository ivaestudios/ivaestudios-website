About Rosewood Mayakoba

Rosewood Mayakoba is a five-star resort tucked inside the Mayakoba complex on the Riviera Maya, Quintana Roo, fifteen minutes north of Playa del Carmen. The property holds 128 suites set along a private Caribbean beach and a network of mangrove channels that thread through the jungle to the sea. Many lagoon suites are reached by skiff, an arrival sequence that has become a signature of the resort.

For weddings and editorial events, Rosewood balances three distinct backdrops — open beachfront, mangrove waterways, and quiet jungle clearings — which lets a single day carry visual variety without long transfers. Notable spaces include the beach club deck for ceremonies, the chapel terrace, the spa overwater pavilion, and Punta Bonita private beach. Capacity ranges from intimate elopements of two through full-property buyouts hosting roughly two hundred guests.

Light + timing at Rosewood Mayakoba

Rosewood sits on a south-facing stretch of the Caribbean. Sunrise reaches the east side of the mangrove first, lighting the lagoon channels with a soft sidelight that holds for roughly forty minutes — the best window for boat portraits. The ocean side carries the strongest sunset light from approximately 6:15 to 6:45 PM depending on season, with the beach club deck and Punta Bonita receiving direct golden-hour at the same moment.

For private resort coverage, Tuesday through Thursday consistently offer the lowest occupancy on the beach and at the chapel terrace. November through April delivers the cleanest skies and calmest water; the shoulder months of May and October tend to give dramatic cloud detail at sunset. We avoid scheduling between 11 AM and 3 PM — overhead tropical sun flattens skin tones and washes out the lagoon water.
