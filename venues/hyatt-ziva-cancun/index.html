About the Property

Family-focused all-inclusive on the peninsula tip

Hyatt Ziva Cancún is a 547-room all-inclusive family resort at km 19.5 on the southernmost peninsula of Cancún Hotel Zone. The peninsula tip is the property's defining feature: waves on three sides, dramatic open horizons, and a sense of being on a private headland that no other Hotel Zone resort can offer.

Best for family vacation portraits, multi-generational gatherings, and small destination weddings of 50-100 guests. The feel is different from the adults-only resorts: energetic, occasionally family-loud, and unmistakably joyful. We schedule sessions early in the morning when the property is at its quietest and the geography is at its most cinematic.

For weddings, Hyatt Ziva's ceremony life centers on the oceanfront wedding gazebo at the peninsula tip, where vows are framed by waves on three sides and a horizon that wraps almost the full view. Smaller ceremonies are also staged directly on the cliff-base beach. Receptions move to the resort's dedicated event terraces and ballroom, and because Ziva is all-inclusive and family-oriented, the celebrations here tend to be warm, multi-generational, and unhurried rather than black-tie. The same peninsula that makes the ceremony dramatic also gives us the strongest portrait location in the entire southern Hotel Zone, so a wedding here and a family portrait session share the exact same landscape. Many couples marry at Ziva precisely because their parents and children are already staying on property.

Getting around Ziva is simple, which matters for families: the resort is compact enough to walk from room to pool to peninsula in a few minutes, and the kids' clubs mean younger children can join a portrait session for a short, well-timed window rather than a long restless one. The property sits at km 19.5, the far southern end of Boulevard Kukulcán, roughly 15 to 20 minutes from Cancún International Airport, which makes it one of the quicker transfers in the Hotel Zone. On the photo-permission side, Hyatt Ziva is an all-inclusive resort with its own events and security protocols, so for sessions involving guests who are not staying on property we coordinate access through the front desk and your wedding planner in advance. We cover Ziva as part of our wider Cancún photography work, and weddings here can add cinematography for the peninsula vows.

A typical session day at Hyatt Ziva, as we plan it, starts at sunrise: we meet at the peninsula tip around 6:30 AM while the property is still quiet, work the three-direction headland and the cliff-base beach for 60 to 90 minutes of family portraits, then finish before the pool noise and midday heat arrive. Wedding days flip the rhythm, building toward a late-afternoon gazebo ceremony with portraits in the warm hour before sunset and a reception that carries into the evening. Families and couples choose Hyatt Ziva because it pairs genuinely kid-friendly all-inclusive logistics with the single most cinematic stretch of geography in Cancún, so the grandparents are comfortable and the photographs still look like a private headland.

Light & Timing

How light moves at Hyatt Ziva

Three-direction peninsula

The peninsula tip lets us shoot east toward the open Caribbean, south down the coast, or west over the Hotel Zone. We choose the angle by season: winter favors south-east, summer favors a north-east frame to keep the sun behind the subject.

Sunrise: quiet and clean

The east-facing main beach is at its quietest weekday mornings before 8 AM. Sunrise (06:30-07:30) is the strongest editorial window before family pool noise picks up. Strongly recommended for portraits.

Sunset

Sunset over the lagoon and southern Hotel Zone gives a backlit silhouette window from the western terraces. We use it for wedding ceremonies and golden-hour family frames.

Crowds & rhythm

Hyatt Ziva runs energetic and family-loud through midday, so schedule formal portraits at sunrise or after sunset. We coordinate with the wedding planner and concierge to lock the gazebo and peninsula tip during your booked window.

How We Work Here

The studio approach at Hyatt Ziva

We treat Hyatt Ziva as a venue we know rather than a backdrop we are seeing for the first time. The single most important decision here is timing: the peninsula is unbeatable at sunrise and chaotic by midday, so we hold your family session in that early window when the headland is empty and the light is soft. Before your date we confirm access with the front desk and, for weddings, pre-clear the gazebo and peninsula tip with your planner and the resort's events team, so the photo team is expected and the schedule is protected.

Our register here is editorial and unobtrusive, with a real talent for keeping young kids and grandparents relaxed: warm, restrained color graded to the open-Caribbean light, candid documentary coverage, and only a few directed portraits where the geography rewards them. Because Ziva runs on the same hurricane-season weather as the rest of Cancún, every plan carries an indoor fallback through the lobby's spiral architecture, which photographs editorially even in rain. The studio is bilingual, led by Director Vianey Díaz, and the full edited gallery is delivered within one to three days for portrait sessions.

What Guests Ask

Hyatt Ziva Cancún, specifically

Does Hyatt Ziva Cancún allow outside photographers?
Yes. Hyatt Ziva Cancún allows outside photographers for guest family sessions and for weddings arranged through the resort's events team. IVAE Studios coordinates access with the front desk and your wedding planner in advance, and for any session that involves guests not staying on property we clear the visit ahead of time. We work within the resort's security and events protocols so you never have to manage access logistics yourself.
What is the best time of day for photos at Hyatt Ziva?
Sunrise, without question. The peninsula tip and the east-facing main beach are at their quietest on weekday mornings before 8 AM, and roughly 6:30 to 7:30 gives soft, clean editorial light before the family pool energy and midday heat arrive. We strongly recommend the sunrise window for portraits. For weddings, the warm hour before sunset works beautifully for ceremony and couple frames on the headland.
Do you charge a travel fee to Hyatt Ziva Cancún?
No. Hyatt Ziva sits at km 19.5 at the southern end of the Cancún Hotel Zone, within our core coverage area, so there is no travel fee for family sessions or weddings on this property. Pricing is by inquiry and depends on the session length, group size, and whether it is a portrait session or full wedding coverage. Send your dates and we reply the same day with availability and a quote.
Can you photograph a large multi-generational family group here?
Yes, multi-generational groups are one of the things we do best at Ziva. We comfortably direct groups of 8 to 30 family members across the peninsula, pool deck, and cliff-base beach, pacing the location flow so younger kids and older relatives stay comfortable through the session. Because the resort is compact and family-friendly, the kids' clubs let us pull children in for a short, well-timed window rather than a long restless one.
How early do you arrive on the wedding day?
For weddings, two of us arrive on property several hours before the ceremony to photograph getting-ready details, scout the day's light at the gazebo and peninsula (it shifts with cloud and humidity), and confirm the resort's setup. Arriving early also means access is already cleared at the front desk and we have located the cleanest portrait angles before the first frame. For sunrise family sessions we meet you at the peninsula at first light.
Can you cover both the ceremony and the reception at a Ziva wedding?
Yes. Full-day wedding coverage at Hyatt Ziva runs continuously from getting-ready, through the gazebo or cliff-base ceremony at the peninsula tip, into the reception on the resort's event terraces and ballroom, and the first stretch of dancing. The property is compact, so the team moves quickly between spaces without losing moments. A second photographer and cinematography are available so the peninsula vows and the reception toasts are both fully documented.
What happens if it rains during our Hyatt Ziva session?
We build every plan with a tested indoor fallback. The lobby's spiral architecture photographs editorially in any weather, with geometric lines and indirect light that read well on camera, and covered terraces give us additional sheltered options. We monitor the forecast in the days before your date, and for sunrise sessions we keep a flexible morning so we can move the call time around a passing shower rather than lose the light entirely.
How soon do we receive our Hyatt Ziva gallery?
For family and portrait sessions at Hyatt Ziva, the edited gallery is typically ready within one to three days, which is fast enough that many families see their photographs before they fly home. Full wedding galleries are delivered within the same week after a careful editing pass, with a small preview shared first. Every gallery is a private, high-resolution online collection with a print release, graded in our warm editorial color.
