About Banyan Tree Mayakoba

Banyan Tree Mayakoba is a five-star resort within the Mayakoba complex on the Riviera Maya, sharing the same protected stretch of jungle and lagoon as Rosewood, Andaz, and Fairmont. The architecture is Asian-influenced — Thai-inspired pavilions, dark teak interiors, lily pools — with 130 villas distributed across the property. Every villa has a private pool, which makes Banyan Tree a strong fit for couples and small ceremonies that want privacy without giving up resort scale.

Notable wedding spaces include the beach club, the wave pool deck, the on-property private cenote (Cenote Buho), and the lobby with its dramatic teak architecture. Capacity ranges from intimate vow exchanges of two through ceremonies of approximately one hundred and fifty guests on the beach club deck. The Saffron and Sands restaurants offer plated dinner options when the brief calls for an architectural backdrop instead of open beach.

Light + timing at Banyan Tree Mayakoba

Banyan Tree's beach faces east, which makes sunrise the headline window — soft Caribbean light builds from roughly 6:00 to 7:15 AM with the resort still mostly empty. Sunset on the beach itself is mostly indirect; for golden-hour color we move to the lagoon-side villa pools or the wave pool deck, where the western light catches the water from approximately 6:00 to 6:45 PM depending on season.

Cenote Buho is open-roof and sits in jungle shade most of the day — the usable sunbeam window where direct light enters the water column runs roughly 11 AM to 1 PM. We schedule cenote portraits inside that window and pair them with a separate beach-or-villa session at golden hour. November through April delivers the most reliable conditions; the shoulder months can produce dramatic sky tones at minor weather risk.
