About One&Only Palmilla

One&Only Palmilla sits on the southern tip of the Baja California peninsula in San José del Cabo. The property opened in 1956 as a private hacienda and has been a flagship destination resort ever since, with 173 rooms and suites distributed across hillside gardens, three pools, and two beaches: the calmer Palmilla Bay and a private cove on the resort's far side. The grounds carry the original Spanish-colonial architecture, including a working bell tower and the iconic Capilla Palmilla.

The chapel is the photographic centerpiece: its sunset ceremony slot has appeared in editorial coverage from Vogue, Hello!, and Travel + Leisure. Beyond the chapel, the Agave Terrace overlook offers a panoramic Pacific frame, the koi pond garden gives an intimate shaded backdrop, and the Suntory restaurant adds an unexpected Asian-aesthetic counterpoint. Capacity ranges from elopements at the chapel's smaller side-altar through full ceremonies for approximately two hundred guests on the Agave Terrace.

For most weddings here the day is built around two signature spaces. Capilla Palmilla, the white-stucco chapel with its working bell tower, holds the ceremony, and because the chapel interior is small and the light through its stained glass is precious, we work it quietly and quickly to protect both the moment and the schedule. Receptions then move to the Agave Terrace, where a long table or a dinner under string lights sits against an open Pacific horizon, or down to the beach for a barefoot evening. The hillside layout means the property reads vertically rather than as a single flat campus, so couple portraits can climb from the koi pond garden up to the terrace overlook in a few minutes, gathering completely different backdrops along the way. It is exactly the kind of luxury destination wedding the studio is built to cover.

On logistics, One&Only Palmilla is a five-star resort with an established events department and strict on-property protocols, so outside photography is arranged through the resort's wedding team and your planner rather than booked cold. We coordinate access, credentials, and the chapel and terrace schedule well in advance, and we follow the resort's quiet-coverage norms during the ceremony and plated dinner service. The property sits at km 7.5 of the Transpeninsular Highway, roughly 20 to 25 minutes from Los Cabos International Airport and ten minutes from San José del Cabo, so guest travel is short and welcome events the evening before are common. We cover Palmilla as part of our wider Los Cabos photography work, and many couples add cinematography so the chapel vows live as both stills and film.

A typical Palmilla wedding day, as we plan it, runs backwards from sunset: getting-ready and detail coverage begins in the suite in the early afternoon; a first look in the koi pond garden or on a quiet stretch of Palmilla Bay lands in soft shaded light; the Capilla Palmilla ceremony is set for the chapel's coveted late-afternoon slot so the recessional spills out into golden hour; couple and family portraits move up to the Agave Terrace for the last warm light over the Pacific; and the reception carries the evening into dinner and dancing. Couples choose One&Only Palmilla for the rare combination of a genuinely iconic chapel, a hillside that delivers half a dozen distinct backdrops within one address, and the quiet competence of a flagship resort, which together make a Los Cabos wedding feel both grand and effortless on camera.

Light + timing at One&Only Palmilla

Palmilla faces the Sea of Cortez at the meeting point with the Pacific. The resort's main beach and chapel are oriented so sunset is the primary photography window: sunrise sits mostly behind the property's hillside and arrives indirectly. Sunset golden hour on the Agave Terrace and at Capilla Palmilla runs roughly 5:50 to 6:35 PM in winter and 6:45 to 7:30 PM in summer, with the chapel's stained glass catching the last light against the white stucco walls.

The two beaches face slightly different directions, which means one carries sidelight while the other catches direct golden hour at the same moment, useful for layering portrait coverage. The strongest months for Palmilla photography run late October through early April: post-hurricane, pre-summer humidity, with consistent clear skies and the sea at its calmest. Mid-summer can deliver dramatic monsoon clouds at sunset for editorial briefs that want texture.

Can you photograph a ceremony at Capilla Palmilla?
Yes, the Capilla Palmilla chapel ceremony is a specialty for us. The chapel interior is small and its stained-glass light is precious, so we work it quietly and efficiently, placing one photographer for the aisle and altar and a second for reactions and the recessional out into golden hour. The chapel also has a smaller side-altar that suits elopements and intimate ceremonies. We coordinate the chapel slot with the resort well ahead of your date.
Can you cover both the ceremony and the reception?
Yes. Full-day coverage at One&Only Palmilla runs continuously from getting-ready in the suite, through the Capilla Palmilla ceremony, into the Agave Terrace or beach reception and the first stretch of dancing. The hillside layout keeps the distances short, so the team moves quickly between the chapel, the garden, and the terrace without losing moments. A second photographer and cinematography are available so the chapel vows and the reception toasts are both fully documented.
How early do you arrive on the wedding day?
Two of us arrive on property several hours before the ceremony to photograph getting-ready details, scout that day's light at the chapel and Agave Terrace (it shifts with cloud and the season), confirm the resort's setup, and start coverage unrushed. Arriving early also means access is already cleared with the events team and we have located the cleanest portrait angles, from the koi pond garden to the overlook, before the first frame.
Do you photograph welcome events and rehearsal dinners at Palmilla too?
Yes. Many destination couples add a welcome party or rehearsal dinner the evening before, often on a Palmilla terrace or beach at sunset, and we cover those candidly as part of the wedding-week story. Booking the surrounding events alongside the wedding keeps one editorial look across the whole trip and a single point of contact. We also cover farewell brunches the morning after, when San José del Cabo light is soft and the group is relaxed.
What is the best season for a One&Only Palmilla wedding shoot?
The strongest months for Palmilla photography run late October through early April: post-hurricane, before summer humidity, with consistent clear skies and the calmest sea. That window is also peak demand, so the chapel and our calendar book early. Mid-summer brings warmth and the chance of dramatic monsoon clouds at sunset, which can read beautifully for editorial briefs that want texture. We plan call times and a weather contingency around your exact date.
