The Session

A family photographer for the desert-meets-sea

As a Los Cabos family photographer, the studio works the one stretch of Mexican coast where the desert runs straight into the ocean. There is no flat tropical sameness here. Instead there are granite cliffs at Land's End, cardón cactus catching the last light, and a Sea of Cortés that turns from cobalt to amber as the sun drops behind the Pacific. We build every family session around that hour, in Cabo San Lucas and San José del Cabo alike, so your portraits carry the specific texture of Baja rather than a postcard that could be anywhere.

The work is editorial and unhurried. We direct gently, in English or Spanish, and we read the room: a two-year-old who needs to run into the surf, a grandfather who would rather sit on the warm sand than stand, a teenager who relaxes the moment nobody asks them to smile. We pace the session so each generation stays comfortable through the golden-hour window, then move quietly between two or three nearby setups. The result is a family that looks like itself, framed against a landscape that looks like nowhere else.

This page is for families specifically. If you are planning a wedding, a proposal, or a couples session in Baja, our broader Los Cabos photographer page covers the Corridor in full. And if your trip also passes through the Caribbean, our luxury family photography service spans both coastlines under one studio.

Where We Shoot

Family photo spots across Los Cabos

01

Médano Beach

The wide, swimmable main beach in Cabo San Lucas, with El Arco sitting on the horizon behind your family. Soft sand for little ones to play, no boat logistics, and the easiest place to fold the Arch into a sunset frame.

02

Palmilla & Chileno Coves

The gentle, protected coves along the Corridor offer calmer water and tide pools that keep young children happily occupied. The signature setting for relaxed, in-the-water family sequences.

03

The Arch by Boat

A private golden-hour panga from the marina puts your family at Land's End with El Arco, Lovers Beach, and the granite cliffs behind. Best for families with older kids and teens who want the dramatic frame up close.

04

San José del Cabo Art District

Colonial streets, bougainvillea walls, and the old mission square give San José a calmer, boutique texture. A strong change of pace from the open beach, and a graceful rain-day or wind-day fallback.

05

East Cape & Estuary

The quieter sands east of San José and the bird-filled estuary offer privacy and uncluttered horizons for families who want to be away from the busier beach clubs entirely.

06

Your Resort

Corridor resorts from Palmilla to Pedregal photograph beautifully: hacienda arches, infinity edges, and palm walkways, all coordinated with your concierge around your dinner reservation.

Light & Timing

How the light moves over Baja

The amber hour

The Pacific-side light in Los Cabos is warmer and more golden than the Caribbean, wrapping families in amber as it reflects off the desert. We meet you about ninety minutes before sunset and shoot through the soft window as the Sea of Cortés calms.

Sunrise for early risers

Families with young, early-waking children often do best at sunrise: near-empty beaches, gentle rose light, and cooler sand. We use it for the calmest sessions and for boat charters to the Arch before the water taxis crowd the cove.

Winter drama

From November through February the Pacific sunsets turn theatrical, and humpback whales breach offshore. We time more sessions to these dramatic evenings, and occasionally a whale appears in the background behind your family.

Tides & crowds

We scout tides and beach-club traffic in advance so your frames stay open and uncluttered. On the Corridor we favor the calmer, swimmable coves over the open Pacific, where the surf is gentler for children and grandparents alike.

What To Expect

Wardrobe, the session day, and what arrives

A Cabo family session is built to feel like the best evening of your trip rather than an appointment. We meet you on a Corridor beach or inside your resort about ninety minutes before sunset, start while there is still room to play in the sand, and move quietly through two or three nearby setups as the light deepens. We direct in English or Spanish, fold in the children's energy instead of fighting it, and keep grandparents seated and close to the action so no one is standing for long. Most families forget the camera is there within the first few minutes.

After booking you receive a wardrobe guide written for the desert-meets-sea palette of Baja. The studio suggests coordinating, not matching, around two or three earthy neutrals: sand, ivory, sage, soft terracotta and warm gold all sit beautifully against granite cliffs and the amber Pacific light rather than competing with it. Flowing linens and cottons move well in the coastal breeze, and barefoot reads better than shoes on the sand. We steer families away from neon, large logos and busy prints, and we are glad to review your outfits before the session so every generation looks like part of the same frame.

The Los Cabos light is warmer and more golden than the Caribbean, and we plan the entire session around it, never the flat, hard light of midday. From November through February the Pacific sunsets turn genuinely theatrical, and humpback whales pass offshore, so a winter session sometimes carries a breach in the background behind your family. You leave with a private online gallery of fully edited, color-graded images in our warm editorial tone, delivered in one to three days, with pricing shared by inquiry so it fits your group and your time on the coast.
