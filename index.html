Chapter 02. A Studio

A studio of one mind, two languages, three coastlines.

IVAE Studios was founded in Cancún in 2023 by Vianey Díaz. a Mexican photographer with a decade behind editorial cameras and a singular conviction: that the most luxurious thing we can offer affluent travellers is not abundance, but restraint.

We do not shoot at noon. We do not photograph fifty families a week. We do not deliver galleries in “four to six weeks.” We work, instead, in the hour the light is honest. and we deliver the work in days, not weeks.

“Luxury is not louder. It is quieter, slower, more deliberate. We’ve built the studio around that single idea.” . Vianey Díaz, Founder
Chapter 02. A Method

The method is old-fashioned. So is the result.

Every IVAE session begins three weeks before the camera does. We learn your itinerary, your wardrobe, your in-laws’ politics. We scout the resort at the exact hour your shoot will run. we know which palm casts a shadow at 06:14 in March, which cenote turns indigo at 17:38 in October.

On the day, we direct in two languages. We move the way an art director moves on a magazine cover shoot. quietly, decisively, without ever telling you to say cheese.

Chapter 02. A Promise

The frame you remember is the frame we remember.

You will receive a private gallery within 72 hours. in most cases sooner. Each frame is hand-edited, never auto-toned, never AI-finished. You will recognise yourself in every image. So will your grandchildren.

That is the IVAE promise. written, signed, and indexed in our terms of engagement.

Chapter 03. A Light Study

Why we shoot only at the edges of the day.

Twenty-four hours of light. Less than ninety minutes of it look the way memory looks. Here is what that hour does. and why every IVAE session lives inside it.

Sunrise window
0min
From the moment the sky turns rose to the moment the light turns flat. We arrive thirty minutes before. We are working when most resorts are still pouring breakfast coffee.
Sunset window
0min
The shorter, more cinematic window. Long shadows, copper highlights, the Caribbean turning a colour you cannot photograph after 18:20.
Color temperature drop
0K → 2,800 K
From clinical noon white to the warm, candle-coloured glow of late golden hour. The frame remembers the difference; you do, too.
