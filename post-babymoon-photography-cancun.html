Journal · Maternity

Babymoon photography in Cancún — the 28-to-34-week window.

A babymoon is three things at once: a slow vacation before the schedule changes forever, a maternity session that travels home as a print, and a stretch of late-pregnancy travel that has to be paced carefully. This is how IVAE Studios builds that one editorial hour around the rest of the trip.

April 2, 2026 · ~7 min read · By Vianey Ortega

A babymoon is the rare trip planned with the next chapter in mind. The vacation comes first — rest, restaurant reservations, late mornings, the spa — and somewhere inside the week sits a single, considered hour with a camera. Of the destinations we work, Cancún is the one we recommend most often for this particular trip. The reason is geography rather than glamour: a Hotel Zone resort puts the airport, the bed, the dinner and the photograph all within a fifteen-minute corridor. Late pregnancy and an hour of driving each way do not mix. Cancún solves that problem.

This is the article we hand clients in their second trimester when they ask whether a babymoon photo session is worth fitting into the trip, when in the pregnancy to schedule it, and how to make it feel like vacation rather than a production day.

The 28-to-34-week sweet spot

The window for a comfortable babymoon and a flattering session is narrower than most couples expect. Before twenty-eight weeks the bump is often still subtle and reads as a slight curve rather than the unmistakable silhouette that makes a maternity photograph feel like a maternity photograph. After thirty-four weeks most international airlines tighten their policies, the body is heavier on its feet, and many physicians prefer travel be kept short. The six weeks in between are the sweet spot — energy is back from the first trimester, the bump is the protagonist of every frame, and travel still feels easy.

We always tell clients to clear the trip with their physician first. Once that is done, we book the session for the early or middle stretch of the trip rather than the last day. A morning of unexpected fatigue is forgivable on day three of a six-day trip; on the final morning it can cost the entire shoot.

The bump is the protagonist of every frame.

Why Cancún, not the Riviera Maya, for a late-pregnancy trip

Both coasts are extraordinary, and we shoot in both. For a babymoon specifically, the Cancún Hotel Zone is the gentler choice. The pace is different. The Hotel Zone is a single seven-mile boulevard between the lagoon and the Caribbean, and most luxury resorts sit fifteen to twenty-five minutes from the airport with no highway driving in between. The room, the spa, the restaurant and the beach session are all within the same property or one short transfer.

The Riviera Maya, by contrast, asks more of the body. Tulum is two hours south of the airport. Akumal is an hour. The cenote and jungle locations that make Riviera Maya extraordinary are also rough access roads, longer transfers, and warmer interior microclimates. None of that is unsafe — couples shoot babymoon sessions in the Riviera Maya every year — but for a third-trimester traveler who wants the quietest possible trip, Cancún wins on logistics.

What changes when the trip slows down

The pace of the trip changes the photograph in ways that most couples do not anticipate. A session photographed at the end of a relaxed three-day stay reads differently than one photographed on a transfer day after a flight. The shoulders sit lower. The breath is slower. The bump is held with both hands instead of clutched. We can see all of that in the gallery the following week. The IVAE call sheet builds around that pace deliberately — we ask which day of the trip, what the rest of the day looks like, and whether dinner is booked after.

Composing a maternity session at the resort

A babymoon session is technically a maternity session that happens to share the trip with a couples vacation. Light, location and direction work the way they do for any IVAE shoot — golden hour, never midday, with a sixty-to-ninety-minute window locked in writing before the deposit. The couple is the second protagonist; the bump is the first. We shape every frame around that hierarchy.

Open shade, not direct beach sun

Late pregnancy responds badly to two things on a tropical shoot: standing for long stretches and direct overhead sun. We solve both with location choice. Resort architecture — covered terraces, archways, palm canopies, infinity-edge pools at sunset — produces soft, even light without the heat load. We sit when the conversation needs to happen and stand only when the camera is up. The shoreline still appears in the gallery, but the open beach is reserved for the calmest stretch of golden hour, not the build-up.

The maternity-and-couples combination

About half of our babymoon clients book the maternity hour as a couples session that happens to feature a bump, rather than a formal pregnancy portrait. Both approaches deliver. The combined session leans on the same editorial direction we bring to a regular Cancún maternity shoot: walking the shoreline, hand on the bump, a quiet shoulder-on-shoulder frame with the Caribbean behind. The couple's relationship reads as the foreground of the story, with the pregnancy as the reason the trip exists. This balance is what most couples actually want and rarely know how to ask for.

Wardrobe for movement and the bump

We send our standard outfit guide adapted for late pregnancy. Long flowing dresses in cream, sand or warm neutrals photograph beautifully and accommodate the bump without pulling tight against the belly. Lightweight fabrics that move with sea breeze rather than fight it. Simple jewelry. Bare feet on the sand. The partner in linen or a soft-shouldered shirt — nothing structured. Anything that bunches across the midsection or competes for visual weight with the bump comes out of the suitcase before the trip.

What the call sheet looks like

Twenty-four hours before the session, every IVAE client receives a one-page call sheet. For a babymoon, that sheet adds two notes: the resort meeting point (so there is no walking from one end of the property to the other), and a fifteen-minute settling buffer at the start. Hair and makeup finish forty-five minutes before arrival. Sunscreen goes on at least two hours earlier so it absorbs without a sheen on the bump. We carry water on every session and pause whenever it is asked for. Nothing about the hour is rushed.

The session itself runs sixty to ninety minutes — long enough to settle, short enough to stay inside golden light, short enough that the body never goes past comfortable. Dinner is booked after. The trip continues at vacation pace.
