Service · 05 / Yacht

On the water, golden hour.

Private yacht photography departing from Cancún or Los Cabos. Marina, open water, sunset. We coordinate with the captain on the light window before booking.

The session

Planned in two halves: a plan and a delivery.

What we plan

Marina or open water — chosen by the look you want, not by the schedule the charter sells. We map the light window per departure: the sunset arc on water runs softer and longer than on a beach, roughly forty-five usable minutes against twenty on land.

Wind contingency is built in. Open-water wind reads heavier than beach wind. The captain and the studio agree on a turn-back point the day before — never on the water.

What we deliver

An editorial mix: deck portraits, open water frames, the departure and the arrival. Curated, not exhaustive — gallery ready in one to three days through a private link.

Cabin and interior frames included when light allows. We never fill a gallery with redundant frames; the cut respects the day.

The charter

We don't book the boat. We coordinate with it.

IVAE Studios is the photography side of the day. The yacht itself is arranged on the client's side, directly with the marina or the charter operator. This keeps the vessel choice — size, crew, catering — fully yours, and keeps our role exactly where it should be: light, timing, direction.

Once the charter is set, we coordinate with the captain on call time, route, anchor points and the sunset window. Most of our clients book through Marina Cancún in the Hotel Zone or Cabo Yacht Charter in Cabo San Lucas — we will share names if helpful.

Send us your travel dates and the size of the party. WhatsApp is the fastest line to the studio.

Investment

A session, plus the marina.

The yacht session is quoted as a base session — couples or family, shaped to the light window agreed with the captain — plus a small travel-to-marina line. The charter itself is billed by the vessel operator, on your side.

Investment upon request. We answer with a written quote inside twenty-four hours, against your travel dates and departure marina.

Where we depart

Two marinas, two registers of light.

Mexican Caribbean

Cancún Marina

Hotel Zone or Puerto Cancún. The Caribbean here reads turquoise into sapphire under late-afternoon sun, and the run to open water — or to the lee of Isla Mujeres — is short. The session is built around a sunset return; cabin frames work best on the way out.

Pacific · Sea of Cortez

Cabo San Lucas Marina

The Pacific side reads cooler and more architectural — the Arch, the cliffs, the Sea of Cortez opening east. Light is cleaner, swell heavier; we plan call time longer before sunset and watch the wind from the captain's forecast, not the dockside flag.

Questions, considered

Four answers, before you ask.

Do we need to book the yacht ourselves?

Yes. Clients arrange the charter directly with the marina or yacht operator — the vessel itself is booked on your side. We coordinate with the captain on call time, route and the light window. Most of our clients book through Marina Cancún or Cabo Yacht Charter; we'll share names if helpful.

Sunrise or sunset?

Sunset is preferred. The arc of light at sea runs softer and longer than on land — roughly forty-five usable minutes against twenty on a beach. Sunrise is possible when the sea is calm and the charter call time fits, and is worth it for couples who want empty water and a cooler, cleaner register.

What if the weather doesn't cooperate?

Open-water wind reads heavier than beach wind. We monitor marine forecasts in the seventy-two hours before departure. If conditions are unsafe — sustained winds above twenty-five knots, swell over four feet, active storm warnings — we reschedule with the captain. Soft overcast is rarely a reason to cancel; it produces some of the best portrait light at sea.

Can we shoot in the cabin or interior?

Yes, light permitting. Cabin and interior frames work beautifully when ports and skylights face the right direction relative to the sun, or in the soft minutes after sunset. We plan that window during the call with the captain so the route puts the light where we need it.
