The Gathering

A whole family, in one place, once in a long while

A reunion is not a regular family session. It is the rare week when three or four generations land in the same place at the same time: grandparents who flew the farthest, adult siblings from different cities, in-laws, and a tier of cousins who only see each other when a trip like this one finally happens. The studio exists to make sure that week leaves something behind, a photograph of everyone, together, that no phone in the group quite manages because someone always ends up behind the camera.

We are a family reunion photographer in Cancun and the Riviera Maya, and most of the gatherings we cover are built around an all-inclusive resort: one property, one home base, everyone under the same roof for several days. That format is what we plan for, big groups, mixed ages, a multi-day rhythm of welcome dinners, pool afternoons and excursions, with one anchored portrait session that gives the family the frames they came for. If your group is smaller or this is a single immediate-family shoot, our Cancún family photography page is the better starting point. If the reunion is built around a milestone, an anniversary, a vow renewal, a destination wedding, see our event and wedding coverage instead.

The studio is led by Director Vianey Díaz, works in English and Spanish through every conversation, and schedules at golden hour so the light flatters every face in a group of thirty at once. We cover the Cancún Hotel Zone, Playa Mujeres, Costa Mujeres and the length of the Riviera Maya.

Why a Reunion Is Different

Built for the large group, not the single family

I

A running order, not a free-for-all

Thirty relatives standing in the sand waiting for direction is how a reunion shoot goes wrong. We send a running order before the date: the full group first while everyone is fresh, then each branch of the family, then the grandparents with the grandchildren, then the cousins on their own. Everyone knows roughly when they are needed, so no grandparent and no toddler stands waiting longer than they should.

II

Group portraits and the days around them

The formal portrait block is the anchor, the photograph the whole family travelled for. But a reunion is a multi-day event, not a single hour. We also cover the candid life of the trip: the welcome dinner, the loud table of cousins, the grandparents watching the kids in the pool, the last breakfast before everyone scatters. Both halves matter, and the unposed ones often matter most.

III

One gallery that travels home with everyone

A reunion sends families back to a dozen different cities, so the deliverable is built to be shared: a private online gallery the whole family can open from anywhere, with a print release so every household orders its own copies. The grandmother in one state and the cousins in another all see the same photographs, the week they were finally in one place.

Across the Stay

What we photograph during a reunion week

01

The full-group portrait

The frame everyone came for: the entire family, all generations, photographed cleanly at golden hour with a horizon that holds behind a large group. This is the anchor of the session and the print that ends up on the most walls.

02

Each branch & the grandparents

Inside the big group are the smaller ones: each individual family, the grandparents with all the grandchildren, the sibling lineups, the cousins together. We work through these in a planned order so every combination is made before the light goes.

03

The welcome dinner

The first night everyone is together: the long table, the toasts, the hugs at the door, the generations meeting the newest baby. The frames that feel like the reunion actually felt.

04

Pool & beach days

The unhurried middle of the trip: grandparents in the shade, cousins who only meet once a year inventing a whole world together in the water. Relaxed, observed, never staged.

05

Excursions & the catamaran day

The big shared outing, a catamaran afternoon, a cenote, a day on Isla Mujeres. The group on the move, where reunions produce some of their most unguarded, joyful frames.

06

The last morning

The final breakfast before the family splits back to different cities. Quiet, a little tired, genuinely close. Often the most honest frames of the week.

How We Plan It

From travel dates to a shared gallery

01 · Before the trip

We learn the family and the group size

You tell us how many are coming, the spread of ages, which resort, and the must-have pairings. We build the running order from that and confirm the golden-hour window for your chosen evening.

02 · On arrival

We coordinate with the resort

We work with your concierge or group planner to lock the portrait location and timing, and scout the light beforehand so a group of thirty is not improvised on the day. We cover Hyatt Ziva Cancún, Le Blanc Spa Resort Cancún, Rosewood Mayakoba and many more.

03 · The portrait block

We move the group through the order, calmly

Full group first, then branches, then grandparents and grandchildren, then cousins. We seat the people who need to sit and photograph the youngest first while their patience holds, and keep the block tight.

04 · After

One gallery, sent to everyone

Preview frames arrive within days, often while you are still on the trip. The full edited gallery follows as a private online album with a print release, so every household, in every city, can order its own copies.
