Why IVAE on the Riviera Maya

One Coastline, Five Wedding Worlds

The Riviera Maya is not one place. It is an 80 mile wedding coastline running south from Puerto Morelos through Playa del Carmen, the Mayakoba resort complex, Puerto Aventuras, Xpu-Ha and Akumal to Tulum, and each stretch has its own light, sand and architecture. A fishing village pier at first light. A five star canal resort at golden hour. A jungle chapel above the Caribbean. A cenote the morning after. We photograph weddings along the entire corridor, and we plan every one around what its exact stretch of coast does best.

Puerto Morelos keeps a bohemian fishing town calm twenty minutes from the airport. Playa del Carmen brings walkable energy, rooftop receptions and the widest resort choice on the coast. Mayakoba is the luxury benchmark, four five star properties on a private canal network that we cover so often it has its own dedicated Mayakoba wedding page. Akumal and Xpu-Ha hold the protected bays with the calmest water for portraits, and Tulum closes the corridor with jungle, boho architecture and the cenotes. If you are still choosing where to marry, our Riviera Maya photographer guide compares the areas in depth.

The Golden Hour Method

Every IVAE wedding gallery is built backward from sunset. We confirm the exact golden hour for your date, place the ceremony sixty to ninety minutes before it, and reserve the last twenty minutes of direct light for the two of you alone. On the Riviera Maya that discipline matters more than any camera: the coast faces east, so mornings give soft pastel light over the water and evenings give warm side light through the palms. We scout your venue at the ceremony hour, mark the frames in advance and guide you through natural movement while the light peaks. It is the reason the galleries read editorial instead of posed.

Bilingual, Local and Fast

IVAE Studios was founded in Cancún by Vianey Díaz, who has spent more than ten years photographing this coast. The team is fully bilingual in English and Spanish, which keeps both sides of a binational guest list at ease, and being local means no flights, no hotel minimums and no travel fees anywhere on the corridor. After the wedding, your complete edited gallery arrives in one to three business days through a private 4K gallery. For couples marrying elsewhere in the country, our destination wedding photography in Mexico page covers the full map, and if your celebration includes a cenote, start with our cenote photographer in Tulum guide.

November through April is the dry season: low humidity, calm seas and predictable golden hours, which is why the corridor's peak wedding calendar runs January through March. May through October brings warmer temperatures and brief afternoon showers that usually clear before sunset, and September and October carry the highest tropical storm risk. Seasonal seaweed (sargassum) varies by year and by beach, so for a May to August date we suggest venues with protected bays such as Akumal, or a cenote or garden backdrop as a backup.
