The Destination

Why couples marry in Cancún

Cancún is built on a 22 kilometer barrier island shaped like a seven, with the open Caribbean on one side and the calm Nichupté Lagoon on the other. That geography is the whole secret: the beach faces east into turquoise water and white coral sand, while the lagoon side catches the actual sunset. A wedding here gets two kinds of light in a single evening, and almost nobody plans for both.

The practical case is just as strong. Cancún International Airport is the best connected in Latin America, so a guest list scattered across the United States, Canada and Europe lands nonstop and reaches the Hotel Zone in under thirty minutes. The resort infrastructure is the deepest in Mexico, from ultra-luxury hideaways at Punta Nizuc to grand all-inclusives that absorb a 200-person celebration without strain. And the sand itself is a photographer's gift: crushed coral that stays cool and reads almost white at golden hour, under a sea that holds five distinct blues in one frame.

IVAE Studios is based in Cancún. This is not a destination we fly into; it is the coast we photograph every week, more than 500 sessions of it since the studio was founded by Vianey Díaz. We know which stretch of each resort's beach clears of loungers first, where the wind drops behind the dune, and exactly when the light turns. The Light

An east-facing beach and a west-facing lagoon give Cancún two golden hours

Cancún's beach faces east into the Caribbean, so the hour before sunset lays soft, even side-light across the sand instead of glaring off the water into the lens. That window, roughly 5:30 to 6:15 p.m. in winter and 6:45 to 7:30 p.m. in summer, is when skin tones glow, the sea saturates and the wind usually settles. It is the single most valuable hour of a Cancún wedding day, and we plan the entire timeline backwards from it.

The island's shape adds a second act most couples never see: while the beach holds that soft eastern light, the Nichupté Lagoon side catches the actual sunset, color burning over still water minutes after the beach portraits wrap. And because the coast faces east, sunrise here is a golden hour of its own, the sun climbing straight out of the sea onto an empty beach. For multi-day coverage we often build in a sunrise session; those frames, made before a single guest is awake, are consistently among the most distinctive in the collection.

This is why IVAE photographs golden hour only. It is not a stylistic quirk; it is the discipline of refusing to make your most important portraits under flat midday glare. For a month-by-month picture of the weather behind the light, see our guide to the best time of year for a Cancún wedding.

What Couples Ask

Cancún weddings, answered

Do Cancún resorts charge an outside-photographer or vendor fee?
Many Hotel Zone resorts have an in-house photo concession and charge an outside-vendor fee for photographers who are not on their list; a few waive it if the photographer is booked as a resort guest for the night. Policies change often and vary by property, so we confirm the current rule at your specific resort before you sign anything and tell you exactly what applies. When a fee exists, the couple pays the resort directly, and we help your planner file the vendor paperwork, our insurance certificate and the team roster ahead of the date. It is routine for us; we work inside these properties year round.
When is the best time of year for a Cancún wedding?
November through April is the dry season: firm skies, lower humidity, predictable golden hours and the calmest sea. May through August brings warmer nights and later sunsets but a higher chance of sargassum seaweed on the open Caribbean coast, which the large resorts clean daily. June through November is hurricane-watch season, with September and October carrying the highest storm risk; for those dates we always confirm an indoor backup ceremony location with your coordinator. December through March Saturdays are the first dates to sell out, both at the venues and on our own calendar. Our month-by-month weather guide goes deeper.
Do we need a permit to get married on the beach in Cancún?
If your ceremony happens at a resort, the hotel handles the beach setup and any municipal requirements as part of your wedding package, and no separate photography permit is needed for us to work at your venue. A legally binding civil ceremony in Mexico requires Registro Civil paperwork, translated documents and in some cases blood tests, which is why most international couples handle the legal signing at home and hold a symbolic or religious ceremony in Cancún. Your resort coordinator or planner manages either path; we simply document it.
How far in advance should we book a Cancún wedding photographer?
IVAE Studios accepts a maximum of 12 weddings a year, so wedding dates close earlier than our portrait calendar does. For high-season Saturdays between December and April, nine to twelve months ahead is realistic; shoulder-season and weekday dates can sometimes be booked four to six months out. We recommend writing to us as soon as your venue contract is signed. If your date is already close, ask anyway; when the calendar allows it, we make short-notice weddings work.
How do we reserve our date, and is there a deposit?
A signed agreement and a booking retainer hold your date exclusively; the remaining balance is due before the wedding day. Once the retainer is in, the date is yours, we do not hold soft reservations or waitlists against it. We accept payment in USD or MXN by card or bank transfer, and the agreement spells out coverage hours, deliverables and timelines so there are no surprises later.
How quickly do we receive our wedding photos?
Your full edited gallery is delivered in 1 to 3 business days through a private 4K online gallery, ready to download, share and print. That is not a teaser, it is the complete edit. Most couples receive their wedding while they are still in Cancún on their honeymoon, which is one of the things our reviews mention most. Print-resolution files are included, and the gallery stays online so family abroad can download from their own devices.
What time is golden hour in Cancún?
Cancún's beaches face east into the Caribbean, so the richest portrait light is the soft, even side-light of the hour before sunset: roughly 5:30 to 6:15 p.m. in winter and 6:45 to 7:30 p.m. in summer. Because the coast faces east, sunrise is a second golden hour here, the sun climbs straight out of the sea onto an empty beach. IVAE shoots golden hour only as a matter of method; we build your ceremony and portrait timeline backwards from that window so the most important frames happen in the best light of the day.

Which Cancún resort photographs best for a wedding?
It depends on the aesthetic you want. Nizuc Resort & Spa at Punta Nizuc is the most editorial property in Cancún, all clean architecture, jungle and private coves. Kempinski Hotel Cancún, the former Ritz-Carlton, is the classic luxury frame with formal gardens and ocean terraces. Le Blanc is adults-only and minimalist; JW Marriott delivers the traditional grand ballroom plus a wide beach; Hyatt Ziva sits on Punta Cancún with water on two sides; Live Aqua reads modern and serene; Hard Rock brings theatrical energy; and the Dreams and Secrets properties are the strongest all-inclusive options. We photograph at all of them and will tell you honestly how your shortlist compares on light.
Do you also offer wedding video in Cancún?
Yes. Photo and film come from one coordinated team, not two studios who have never met. The photographers and filmmakers share one timeline and agree positions in advance so neither crew appears in the other's frames, and the edits are matched in color and mood. Your planner adds a single studio to the resort vendor manifest instead of two. See our Cancún wedding videographer page for how the film side works and what a highlight film includes.
Can you cover our welcome dinner or a next-day session too?
Yes, and in Cancún it is usually worth it. Most destination couples host a welcome dinner or beach party the night before, and the east-facing coast makes a sunrise or next-day session one of the most distinctive chapters in the collection: empty beach, glassy water, no guests. Many couples pair the wedding day with a trash-the-dress or cenote session a day later. We structure multi-day coverage from one timeline so every event is lit and sequenced deliberately rather than bolted on.
