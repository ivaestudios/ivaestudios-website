Creative Director & Lead Photographer

Vianey Ortega

Founder of IVAE Studios. She works from Riviera Maya, photographing families, couples and destination weddings at luxury resorts across the Mexican Caribbean and the Pacific — bilingual, native to the place, deliberate with light.

Vianey Ortega leads IVAE Studios from Riviera Maya, México, where the studio specialises in editorial photography for international travellers staying at the region’s luxury resorts. Her work moves between the Mexican Caribbean and the Pacific coastline of Los Cabos, with a register that has more in common with hospitality magazines than with conventional vacation photography. The studio’s clients are guests at the resorts she has worked with the longest — and they arrive expecting direction, not improvisation.

The studio was founded on a small, specific gap: travellers were paying premium nightly rates at properties of unimpeachable design, then leaving with photographs that looked like everyone else’s. Vianey set out to make work that matched the room — the architecture, the staffing, the intention behind a five-night stay — rather than the souvenir industry built around it. The shift was slow and deliberate: one resort, one family, one referral at a time.

Her direction is editorial in the strict sense. Sessions are scheduled to a single light window — golden hour, never midday. Locations are scouted ahead, not chosen on the walk. Wardrobe is discussed before the dates are confirmed, because a linen palette will read on the page where a logo will not. Galleries are culled tight, not generous; she would rather deliver eighty considered frames than three hundred with three repeats of every pose. The standard is consistency across the gallery, not the highlight reel.

Direction over moments.

Most of her year is spent photographing families at all-inclusive and boutique resorts — second-generation guests, often the third or fourth visit to the same property — and couples on anniversary, honeymoon or vow-renewal stays. Destination weddings she takes selectively, with a preference for intimate ceremonies where the day can be directed in full rather than chased between vendors. Maternity, yacht and cenote sessions appear less often, but with the same governing logic: a single light, a clear concept, and a brief written before anyone meets at the lobby.

She works in English and Spanish without translation. For most clients flying in from the United States or Canada, that means direction comes in their language; for the resort staff who let her quietly stage a balcony at 6:42 a.m., it means the courtesy comes in theirs. The studio does not list prices publicly. Investment is shared on enquiry, after travel dates and resort are confirmed — because that is what determines the brief, and the brief is what determines the price.
