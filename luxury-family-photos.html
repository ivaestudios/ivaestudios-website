Service · Family

Editorial direction for the trip you actually planned.

Family sessions for travelers who want photographs that feel editorial, not posed. Riviera Maya · Cancún · Los Cabos. Sixty to ninety minutes of directed time, golden hour only.

The session

Sixty to ninety minutes, golden hour, directed.

What we plan

  • The light window for your stay — sunrise or sunset, mapped to the day with the calmest weather forecast.
  • A location walk-through inside your resort or within a fifteen-minute radius, scouted in advance.
  • An outfit guide tailored to the chosen location: tones that read together, fabrics that move in coastal wind.
  • Wind and crowd contingency — a second composition pre-planned in case the first turns busy or breezy.

What we deliver

  • A curated gallery — we don't deliver every frame. The cull is part of the work.
  • Color graded to read as a single editorial set, not a collection of unrelated favorites.
  • One to three business days from session date, delivered to a private password-protected gallery.
  • Print and web sizes side by side, ready for framing, sharing, or a tasteful holiday card.

Frequently asked

What families ask first.

How long is the session?

Sixty to ninety minutes inside golden hour. The window includes a brief setup walk-through with you on arrival and the actual shoot — directed in real time, not improvised. We don't run over light. Once the sun crosses the threshold the texture changes, and a tired family in flat light is a worse trade than the photograph we already have.

Can children be young?

Yes — newborns to teens. We pace the session to the youngest in the group, which usually means quieter directions, fewer transitions, and snack breaks built into the timeline. Extra time isn't extra discipline; we keep the window. A good family photograph from a baby's twenty-minute happy stretch beats an exhausted hour.

How much does it cost?

Investment is quoted per trip. Length, location, group size and travel-time all shift the structure, so a single number on a page would mislead. Send your travel dates and the resort you're staying at, and we respond within twenty-four hours with availability and a tailored quote. We close one session per golden-hour slot, so the calendar fills earlier than most photographers'.

Where is best for a family?

Resort beach at golden hour for most — direct light, low crowds, and an easy walk back to the room. Open shade on a deck or under a portico if a baby is in the group, since direct sun is hard on the youngest faces. Cenotes are off-limits for very young children: limestone steps, cool water, low light. We'll guide the choice once we know who is in the group and where you're staying.
